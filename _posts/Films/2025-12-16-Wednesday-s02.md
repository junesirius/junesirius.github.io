> Because even though Wednesday is literally the tunnel at the end of my light, I can't imagine my life without her in it.
>
> -- Enid Sinclair

I see destiel in there

I see gelphie in there

I see WildeHopps in there

and now I have Wenclair

I mean, what's the chance that all the ships can devour me like they are one? there's always one (or both) sacrificing themselves for another, always some breakup arc and heartbroken (or worse, widower arc), someone almost dying, someone scared to shit, always some choosing the other over themselves, and love too much but not realizing the feeling...

I mean, I didn't watch many shows or movies this year, let me see, this year it's just spn (for the 100th time), Wicked 2, Zootopia 2, and now just finished Wednesday 2, and ALL OF THEM manage to make me cry at least once and remind me of the other ships? what's the issue with me? now I have to wonder if there's something deeper in me that made me keep looking for and getting attracted by the same story again and again...

(no actually, I have the answer already, the story of those who don't belong and are lonely, those who still care and love, sometimes too much, those who don't believe they'll be loved back and accepted, but eventually there's always someone who does, someone who see you and love you back, or even more, or die loving. no matter how different you are, they understand, and they accept, and they love. I feel like I'm just trying over and over again to heal myself in those stories, trying to make myself believe these aren't just stories, that one day I'll be seen and loved like those stories do. that's why I too am writing the same type of story over and over again, in different worlds, on different characters, but always the same core, because that's what I am. that's what I'm waiting for. that's what I'm looking for.)

<br>

Rewatch S01-02 notes: the false accusation really hurts. S01 when she accuses Xavier to be the Hyde and manually frames him and sends him to jail, and then accuses her therapist to be the Hyde master, she made two wrong accusation in the same day (the only right part is she figured out there are two), and the doctor dies as the next victim immediately and Xavier is really really hurt. Wednesday can't always just get away with hurting people as if she really doesn't have to care about the consequence. Even Wednesday Addams needs friends. But glad Xavier chooses to forgive her because how she reflexively intuitively jumps out to save him from an arrow. But it's also very understandable that, even if it's not the actor's problem, as a character, Xavier wouldn't want to continue in this school or in this community or just anywhere near this area, Wednesday hurt him too deeply, and bad things couldn't always get cancelled out by the good things that come after. Even with all the right things Wednesday did, she still did too much wrong and hurt too many people, especially those who are the closest to her.

Love how the story always have multiple plotlines intertwined together, more than one crime, more than one villain, and yet somehow all those plots can weave together to form a bigger and more complete conspiracy, which always related to some dark hidden Addams family secret.

S01 has the Hyde storyline (Tyler Galpin and Laurel Gates), the Goody and Joseph Crackstone storyline from 400 years ago, Garrett Gates and Morticia/Gomez's case, the conflict between normie and outcasts, the underlying Morningsong crime. Tied together by the prophecy of Wednesday saving/killing the school, and the very eventful villain motivation and methods: Because of Morticia/Gomez's causing Garrett's death, Laurel uses Tyler to resurrect Joseph Crackstone to attack outcasts, all because of the same hatred toward the outcasts, and defeated by the same courageous young Addams, Wednesday with her spirit guide Goody, defeating the old enemy from 400 years ago all over again, history replay.

S02 has the Hyde storyline again (Tyler Galpin and Laurel Gates), the LOIS program (Judi and Augustus Stonehurst and Francoise Galpin and then merge with Tyler's), the zombie boy ("Slurp"/Isaac Night)'s original story (and then merge with Tyler's), and Morticia/Gomez's old story.

There're always at least 3-4 different storylines moving in parallel, no wonder all the mysteries and suspensions are so difficult to crack, it's just too easy to mix the evidence and crime scene together and make mistakes. The mystery-solving part is probably the most interesting part of this show. Thinking about it, Stranger Things season 1 is almost also like this, 3-4 storylines, putting them together slowly, sometimes turns out to be different forces, sometimes can converge, certainly a huge challenge on story-building and writing.

About the open ending for the season 3, looking very obvious now the story isn't over, Aunt Ophelia and Grandmama part is still a big secret. Addams family certainly has its share of dark secret. The one-eyed raven is still flying around even though Judi Crackstone was dead, maybe whoever the original outcast was that Augustus stole the ability from to transplant onto her daughter was still alive or reactivated? And of course the most popular post-S2 fanfic topic about how to find Enid and helps her turn back from the werewolf form, of course Wednesday would find her like she promised and help her. This is probably the least concern right now.

And this gives a thought about story ending. Even as an open-ending like S2's ending, you know something isn't done, but all of us feel comfortable and confident that Wednesday won't let her down and this will be fixed, this is a way better and more hopeful ending than most of the "happy ending" the many real show finale tried to come up with, when the writers thought the only way is to tie all the knots and make them the happy future a settled future, but there never can't be a "single one happy future" that would fit everyone's vision, so those endings are almost always considered bad writing. Maybe writers need to change the mindset about finale. Instead of closure, the real finale should also be open. Hannibal did well on that.
