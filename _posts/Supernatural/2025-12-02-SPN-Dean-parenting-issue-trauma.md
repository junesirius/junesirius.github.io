was just reading a post about dad!Dean take care of Jack, and he gets quietly gradually healed by the kid and the process, and realizes he's become a father and he's nothing like John Winchester. That touched me somewhere so deep and reminded me of something I keep thinking.

<p style="color: #0000; text-indent: 2em">I feel for Dean so deeply for never feeling ready to have a kid of his own, and Bobby too, for that matter, scared of messing things up, scared they'd be really bad at it. Growing up in an abusive family has that effect on you. I would know, because, well, I grew up in a very similar A+ parenting environment. Maybe not as bad as John Winchester, but so close that John reminds me too much of my own father, all those anger control issues, the depression, the beat-up and punishment when I did something wrong. I always thought it was just me not doing things right, and I tried so desperately to get approval, and you know the rest of the story already -- I don't want kids, I don't want marriage, and I mess up a lot in my relationships, never had one for longer than three months (but I do have many good friends). Because I'm so scared I'd be really terrible at parenting, because mine are the only ones I really learned parenting from, and I don't know if I can ever do better. What if I become just like them; what if I did one thing wrong and traumatized a kid for their entire life; what if it was my fault that one way or another, I'd ruin their life. I just can't do this to them. I just can't think about having my own kid. So yeah, I feel for Dean, and I feel for Bobby.</p>

I guess that's also another reason why SPN means so much to me. Besides destiel, there're so many stories about hope and healing right there, right where I needed healing, telling me it's possible to find someone who'd love you unconditionally (even though I don't know how it feels like yet), telling me it's possible to grow out of that kind of childhood trauma (that it is a trauma to begin with, I didn't see it until becoming grownup). That Dean can have Jack and Claire and be a really good father figure to them, that Bobby can have the boys and know exactly how to be a father to them. It means so much to me. Maybe that's why my stories are round and round writing the same theme, trying to repeat the same healing process all the time too. Trying so desperately, compulsorily to heal that lonely, scared, messed-up kid inside me. Maybe one day I could finally heal myself too. Maybe one day, I could be lucky enough to run into my angel too. Maybe.

I just have to keep the hope up.
