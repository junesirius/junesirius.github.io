"Your skills have improved, Dean." The doctor inspects the patient lying on the operating table, as Dean wipes his blade on his coat. The white gown has turned mostly red, with all the blood splashing on it.

"I still have much to learn, Master," Dean answers, turning back with a smaller precision knife.

"You just need some more practice, that's all. You only did it for ten years; give it another ten years, you'll be as good as me." The doctor pats his shoulder encouragingly, pointing at the edge of the patient's head. "See here? You still hesitated when you cut in, distracted by their crying. You need to focus. When you operate on them, you can't be bothered by those screams; you have to enjoy them, you have to finish your job. In fact, the more they scream, it means the more effective your work is, it means you're doing it right."

"Yes, Master," Dean answers obediently, studying the patient's half-cut skull and the flesh and liquid dripping out that used to be part of the brain.

"Go on, continue. You know what's next -- cut out their brain. Lobotomy is always the best part. It's so beautiful, it's more art than science." His master urges, standing next to the table with folded arms.

The patient is crying some nonsense, cursing them, calling them demons, screaming nonstoppingly, the usual -- Dean blocks out the noise, focusing on the task, the fun part. He knows he has to hurry, or the patient will just pass out in pain -- it will be useless to operate without them feeling anything, according to the doctor.

He cuts into the opened skull, all the way through the brain structure, watching the blood and flesh split apart under his knife. The patient twitches on the operating table involuntarily, until slowly the move dies down and the scream quiets.

Dean glances at his master nervously with a frown. "Is it too deep?"

The doctor rounds to his side of the table, speculating on the cut he just made, taking over his knife. He gestures for Dean to look closer, and cuts into the brain himself. Fresh blood gushes out, streaming down his gloved hand.

"If you cut two inches to the left, stabbing in right through here," he instructs while operating. "This can cut out most of the memory, but leaves the emotions and pain sensors still sensitive. So they won't remember who they are, what they're doing, or anything at all, but they'll still feel it. They won't pass out yet. And if you patch their skull back up like this, they can still walk and talk, might not even realize what's happened to them, making them the perfect little puppy. You just have to do it right."

"I'm sorry." Dean lowers his eyes. "I remember you showing me, but I can't seem to get it right."

"You're the best student I've ever seen," his master comforts him, taking out the knife and licking clean the blood dripping from the blade with his tongue. "Do you trust me?"

"Of course, Master," Dean answers quickly, looking up.

"And you're having fun?"

"Yes."

The doctor smiles. "Good," then yells in the door's direction. "Next!"

Two nurses open the door and dash in, wheeling the passed-out patient out, and another two push in the next one, cuffing and tying it up on the operating table. The screams from other rooms permeate the air in the corridor, passing into the room during the short break when the door is opened. Dean hears them, but he's already got used to it.

"How many operations are out there?" he asks, as he turns to the tool table, examining his knives and tools, getting ready for the new operation.

"Hundreds of thousands," the doctor answers simply.

"And all these people," Dean gestures to the new one lying there, who's struggling and crying even louder than the last one, wailing for mercy. "They all deserve it? The operation and the punishment?"

The doctor narrows his black eyes, thinning into a line. "Are you having feelings for them, Dean? Do you prefer to be the one lying there, instead?"

"No," Dean answers hastily, picking up the biggest knife on the table, grabbing its handle tight in his hand, walking toward the frightened patient. One tip of his lips curls up with joy and expectation of what's coming next. "I don't know why I asked that."

He buries the knife into the patient's skull. The crying turns into a desperate painful howl.

Dean watches the blood pour out of the cutting line, smelling the sweet taste of blood suffusing the whole operating room. Light of excitement glints in his black eyes. He grins wider. "Of course, they deserve this; they deserve all the pain. I'm doing them a favor."

He cuts a second time, a deeper line. Half the blade is buried inside the skull before he takes it out. His hands are not shaking.

"How did I do this time, Master Alastair?"

Alastair smiles with a hint of pride. "Attaboy."
