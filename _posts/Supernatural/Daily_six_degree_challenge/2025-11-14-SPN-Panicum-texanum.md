**Scene:**

"Cas, come on, there gotta be a way to do this more efficiently," Dean straightens up, rubbing the small of his back, feeling the age not treating him kindly. His fingers are covered with soil and grass leaves, the bucket of weeds waiting by his feet. Cas is a few steps ahead of him, working on the left side, and he is tasked with handling the right side.

Cas pauses and turns back to him, a small cluster of grass shaking between his fingers as he rubs them gently -- called panic grass or something, according to Cas. "If you're tired, you can get inside to rest. I can finish the remaining land alone."

Dean glances around them. There are still acres after acres of farmland around them. Why did he agree to buy the land and suddenly become a farmer again? Oh, right, it felt like a good decision at the wedding.

"I'm not tired, just... bored," Dean grumbles quietly, wiping the sweat from his forehead. He forgets about the soil in his hands, which mixes with the sweat and makes his face itchy. Cas walks over to him, dropping the grass, wiping his forehead and cheek with his thumbs. Dean can feel the ring on his finger brush gently against his skin. The aching around the waist and knees is soothed.

"How about now?" Cas looks into his eyes, smiling small.

"Better," Dean smiles back, pecking his lips lightly. It tastes salty. Blame the sweat and the weather. "Why can't you magic the land free of the weeds?"

"I can," Cas considers, glancing at the field briefly. "But I *want to* do things the human way. I want to feel it. I want to do it the way you would. The proper, real way."

Dean is muted for a second, feeling his chest swell and shrink, like rides rising and falling. He tries to ease his breath, and Cas distracts him by kissing him.

"We can use the weed killer or something," Dean rests his forehead against his husband's, still thinking.

"That would be harmful to the soil, the crops, and the insects." Cas reasons. "Really, go inside, rest for a while. You can see me from the house anyway."

Dean raises his brows. "How do you know?" Yeah, one of the reasons he didn't want to leave Cas out here working alone is that he just wants to watch him work.

Cas just smiles. "Because I'm doing the same thing all the time, finding it impossible to keep my eyes off you."

Dean laughs. "We're so hopeless." *I'm so hopelessly in love.* That's what he means.

Cas chuckles slightly, too. "Sam said the same every time he visited, since ten years ago. I'm thinking about using the vegetable in our field to make him a fresh salad next time he comes, so he can stop grinning like we being together is still news to him. The spinach should be ready -- if we can finish all the remaining work."

"Alright, alright, I hear you," Dean grudgingly lets his husband go, not forgetting to leave another peck on the corner of his lips before they go back to the weeding task. "You're spoiling him even worse than I ever do."

"I heard it's not uncommon for humans to please their in-laws," Cas answers, moving back to the spot he left, picking up another long-leaved grass and dumping it inside the bucket.

"I'll kick his ass if he ever even thinks about not behaving around you," Dean answers with a small grin.

Sam makes an unconscious sneeze a few hundred miles away.
