**Scene:**

(**Warning**: including John Winchester's A+ parenting)

<br>

The small town in Wisconsin is witnessing a concerningly increasing amount of missing people reports, most of them deer hunters, during the deer hunting season.

Garth is the one who called them. "I sniffed something weird. Our kind of weird," he says over the phone.

"'Sniffed'?" Dean raises his eyebrows, even though Garth can't see. "Like using your super wolf power? Is that a werewolf case?"

"Yes and no," Garth says, his voice muffled and distracted. "I think it's some kind of creature, but I don't think it's werewolves. Can you help check it out? I would have if I didn't need to babysit two new pups."

And Dean has to say yes.

<br>

Dean and Cas scout the woods where the last missing person was witnessed. Sam and Eileen go to the police station to hopefully gather some more information.

They run into a deer.

The creature doesn't run away, but just stills, turning to look in their direction, black eyes staring at them.

Dean thinks their eyes meet. His heart leaps in that moment. The eyes are powerful, alive, and very, very expressive. He feels as if he's forced into a conversation on the soul level with the creature, and he can't breathe. And for some reason, this feels too familiar.

He reaches a hand to stop Cas from moving.

And he can't help holding his breath, his fingers tightening around the gun in his hand.

The deer chews the high grass around it peacefully, and strolls away at a slow pace after a short while.

"Huh," Dean says quietly, looking in the direction the deer moved away. "Does that look weird to you? Deer in a hunting season, but not afraid of hunters at all?"

Cas glances at him briefly. "He might not realize we carry a gun. In fact, he might not notice us at all."

Dean hums, lost in thought a little. "Do you know Bambi there was the first creature Dad asked me to kill?" he blunts out suddenly, lips twitching into a failed attempt of a bitter smile.

Cas turns to him fully, eyes concerning. "What happened?"

Dean avoids his eyes, just staring at the spot in the short distance where that deer was. "He was teaching us shooting, and we needed moving targets. He told us those deer were infected," he lets out a humorless huff then, "with werewolf virus, which effectively makes them... were-deer? I guess. So we should shoot them."

"I do not think that is how werewolf infection works, or this new monster-creature existed," Cas narrows his eyes.

Dean chuckles humorlessly. "They don't, but we didn't know back then. Dad just wanted us to pull the trigger on something. Because I couldn't do it back then, not yet." He shakes his head slightly.

"How old were you?"

"Eight, I think."

"Dean," Cas sighs, putting a hand on Dean's shoulder, squeezing slightly.

Dean shakes his head again, trying to let out a breath that's held inside the tight chest.

"What happened?" Cas asks quietly.

"We ran into a deer, just like the Bambi here. And when I aimed the creature in my crosshair, I saw its eyes. Those big black eyes, looked right at me, through me, like they saw me, they knew I was there, and I was about to kill them. And I... I've never killed anything before that; I just couldn't do it. It's taking a life, Cas..." Dean's voice starts breaking.

Cas doesn't answer. He can't. He can already guess the rest of the story. How many times has he heard someone calling Dean a killer? How many lives and monsters has he seen Dean kill? But no one is born with a kill count. They always start somewhere.

Cas wraps his arm around Dean's shoulder, giving him half a hug. Dean leans in, resting some of his weight against Cas's shoulder. His breath evens a little.

Cas doesn't ask what happened next, but Dean continues anyway. "I can still hear what Dad said that day, as clear as yesterday." His voice is even lower than usual. "He said, 'I didn't raise you to be a namby-pamby. Now, get rid of this wishy-washy sentimental nonsense and pull the bloody trigger. You're a killer, Dean, you kill monsters, and right now, this is what I'm telling you to kill. So do it.'"

His shoulders sag, his fingers only hold loosely around the gun, which suddenly seems too heavy.

"You're not, Dean," Cas says firmly. "You hear me? Refusing to kill and having feelings for lives doesn't make you weak. And you're not what your father said you were. You're *not* a killer."

Dean huffs bitterly. "Yeah, right, tell that deer that day, killed by a bloody eight-year-old. And everyone died because of me."

"That's not your fault. None of that is. You have to see that, Dean." Cas holds him in a proper embrace now. Dean allows himself to be held for longer than he would usually do.

"Well, it doesn't matter now. What's done is done." Dean pulls away eventually, wiping the corners of his eyes carelessly.

"With your permission, I would very much like to kill your father again," Cas grits his teeth.

Dean chuckles quietly, this time genuinely. "Come on, we should go. If were-deer didn't do that, then we still have a deer-hunter-killer out here. We'll have to catch it before it takes out more people." He pats Cas on the back once, then resumes to investigate and move into the deeper part of the woods.

<br>

But as it turns out, were-deer is actually real. It's nothing like what they thought, and certainly not what John Winchester claimed it to be; but it's indeed responsible for the case and disappearance populations -- something about revenge. But Dean and Cas silently mutually reach an agreement, this does not make John Winchester's behaviors any more acceptable than they were.
