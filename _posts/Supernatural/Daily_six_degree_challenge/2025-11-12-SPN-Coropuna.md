**Scene:**

"Holy mother of -- " Dean grunts and curses, stumbling to a halt, catching his breath. "I need a break. Jesus, how high are we now?"

Sam squints to check the altimeter on his smart watch, which is now covered with a layer of frost. Cas answers before he can get a read: "About 20 thousand feet high now, we have another thousand to go."

"Jesus," Dean grunts again, feeling his chest tight for the lack of oxygen at such a height and low temperature. He tries to breathe slowly. Snow covers everything in their sight. "I would never know the first time I get to this part of the sphere, I'd be here freezing my ass off. Isn't life always a wonder, eh?"

"What are you imagining? We're here for the job, not for vacation. Cas is the one who told us unless this muki creature's true form is put to rest, it can't be killed, and it will never cease to hunt its target. And who is to blame that they just decide to live on the Coropuna, just the highest mountain top in Peru!" Sam grumbles, almost tripped by a huge piece of rock on his way to get some rest.

"Actually, it's the third highest in Peru," Cas corrects him while helping him steady his steps.

"Like that makes a difference." Dean moans, glancing upward. There is just more white before them, not much else to see. He can't feel his hands or legs since hours ago.

"What am I imagining?" Dean starts, just trying to distract his brain to think about something else, something warmer, something better. "We are in Peru! And I googled it, and it's right next to the Ocean! I was thinking beach and sunshine, cute chicks and sexy accents, bonfire dances and a lot of booze. I've never been here, and I skipped all the geology class hunting! How would I know?"

"Dude, you're probably thinking about California." Sam chuckles slightly.

Dean rolls his eyes. "As soon as this is done -- I mean, as soon as -- I'll go to the beach. I deserve this. We all deserve this." He tries to rub his hands, but across the glove, this doesn't really help create much heat. "God, how could *anything* live somewhere like this?"

Sam murmurs an agreement.

"It's not always this cold." Cas frowns at the mountain tip through the low cloud and heavy snow caps, telling them. "Coropuna is a dormant volcano, so when the volcano erupts, the temperature will increase. But it hasn't happened for two thousand years."

"And if we happened to be up there, we'll be toasted like a Thanksgiving turkey. Yeah, nice and warm, but no thanks." Dean tries to squeeze out a smile, but the muscle on his cheeks also feels frozen. He rubs his face tiredly. "So it's either too cold or too hot. God, I hate this place. I miss my bed and the bunker's hot tub."

"Here," Cas walks up to him, his fingers brushing Dean's forehead briefly.

A thin thread of grace sinks through his skin.

"What did --" Dean is slightly startled, but words are stifled when the sense of warmth and comfort flows through his veins, warming him like the best hot tub in the world.

"Holy shit, that feels so good," he can't help exclaiming, staring at the angel with gratitude. "I never knew grace could be used like that!" He flexes his hands, having the feelings back again like a newly awakened bear.

"You're welcome," Cas watches him with a small smile.

"Hey, guys?" Sam stumbles toward them clumsily. "Mind give me a dose, too? It's really freezing my balls off."

Cas is about to turn to him, but Dean puts a hand on his shoulder with a small laugh, pulling him back. "Get in the line, I'm not done yet." He gives Sam a pointed wink.

"Jerk," Sam pouts unhappily, then grumbles quietly. "He's your boyfriend, who am I to argue?"

Dean hears him, but he is suddenly in too good a mood to retort.
