**Meaning:**

n. (or *phragmocone*) the thin conical chambered internal shell of a belemnite that is either straight or curved, is produced in front into a very thin process resembling a blade or leaf, and fits behind into a deep cavity in the anterior end of the guard (*from Merriam-Webster Dictionary*)

the chambered portion of the shell of a cephalopod. It is divided by septa into camerae. (*from Wikipedia*)

**Scene:**

The natural history museum has a lost fossil case. It's all over the newspaper.

"Call the police, then, or detectives, or real FBI, anything. How come it's our kind of thing?" Dean steps out of the car, smoothening his tie, glancing back at Cas and Sam.

"The fossil is a phragmacone of an ammonite, believed to have some connection with the newly released movie, *Ammonite*," Sam reads from his phone.

"What's that phrag-something, and ammo-something word? I feel like a five-year-old learning English right now. Do they really have to make words that only like ten people in the world know and five actually use them?" Dean bitches.

"Terminologies are necessary to be accurate." Cas chimes in before Sam can open a new browser on his phone to find the answers. "They're a very specific and delicate structure of an extinct species, a thin chambered portion divided by septa into camerae."

Dean raises his eyebrows. "Not helping, Cas. Now I've just become like a three-year-old."

Sam sighs. "Sort of a shell of a dead squid from dinosaur time."

"Finally someone speaks English," Dean gives him an approving nod. "But still not seeing how it's our kind of case. Call the biologist, or geologist, or I don't know, some sort of scientist."

They almost reach the door of the museum now. Dean pauses just out of the yellow line that says "line start here", turning around to face his brother and the angel.

"Someone believed it's not stolen, but escaped." Sam tries very hard not to laugh, but his lips twitch at the words.

"Escaped." Dean stares at him like his brother suddenly grows a second head. "Like the rock suddenly grew some legs and the squid just ran away at night? Wait, does squid even have legs?" He turns to Cas with questioning eyebrows.

"No -- yes -- depends on how you define the legs." Cas starts, but Sam reaches out a hand to stop his palaeobiological lecture right there.

"It's not an illusion, I checked the camera footage, the fossil indeed appeared to be moving on its own. People are scared it might be possessed and demonic, and eventually, ominous and murderous." Sam turns his phone around to show them the footage.

Dean watches it with open amusement. "Alright," he says slowly. "What are we thinking? Haunted? Cursed? Don't tell me it's like a ghost squid having unfinished business from -- how many years ago is the dinosaur time? Are they even that intelligent to have a soul or something?"

"Two hundred million years, give or take." Cas answers, completely not aware if those are questions that really need answering. "And yes, they're very intelligent. I was there, I know."

Dean gives him a proper stare. "Are you just saying that to show how old you are?"

Cas stares back. "You've always known I'm extremely old."

"Yeah, but not like, you're the same age as dinosaurs or something."

"Actually, I'm way older than dinosaurs."

"Stop, you two." Sam has to stand between the two of them, pulling them apart. "What's the matter, Dean? Can't handle the age difference or what? It's not like Cas lied about his age before you guys started dating."

Dean rolls his eyes. "I don't even know where to start. How can you possibly use one sentence to offend both of us? I'm not like a teenage girl dating some super duper weird old guy."

Cas blinks. "Some people did consider me a 'super duper weird old guy'." He air-quotes.

Dean rolls his eyes harder. "You know what I mean. And no, I don't have a problem dating one. Are we all good? Can we focus back on the case now?"

"Oh, I thought you'd never say that," Sam tries hard enough to hide his laugh, making a gesture to let Dean lead the way toward the museum gate.

<br>

As it turns out, the several eons of knowledge that Cas possesses, which happens to cover the entire history of every fossil and the lost phragmacone in question in the museum, does come out quite handy to help with the case, especially when communicating with the nerdy museum guides. Dean mentally makes a note that there are indeed more than ten people in the world who would be using those other terminologies that he doesn't even know exist or would have a word for.

Except the case itself ends up pointing to have a lead, not connected to the shell or squid or any extinct species, but like Sam noticed very early on, to the newly aired movie *Ammonite*, that just so happened to be very cinematically homosexual.

Dean shifts a lot in his seat when they go through the movie to check what could possibly be the cause that is seemingly resurrecting the fossils. And he glances at Cas a lot, during and after the movie.

"That's a beautiful movie," he sighs as it starts rolling credit. "Is it just me, or those girls in the movie are just so Cas?"

"They are very knowledgeable of their individual fields; if that's what you mean, then I guess, yes." Cas considers.

"I think he means they're very hot and desperately in love, just like the two of you," Sam blunts out, pointedly looking at Dean when he answers. "That's exactly what you want to hear, aren't you?"

Dean blushes but smiles goofily, patting his brother's shoulder. "Finally for once I can see why they say you're smart."

"And I can see why they say you're... forget about it," Sam drops the sentence after he gives the other two a quick glance. "I'm back to work, you two, I don't know, do whatever you do when I'm here. Hang the socks on the door if you want to... do the not-fossil-related things like the ladies in the film."

Dean gives him another goofy smile as a reply.

<br>

The case with the fossil, at the end of the day, turns out to be the exact piece that becomes evidence and a showcase of the deep love between the British paleontologist and the geologist, just like the film shows, so deep that there must be a piece of soul from both of them attached to it, giving it a resemblance to life.

The museum strongly protests the salt-and-burn solution, so Cas has to make a trip to the Upstairs, actually seeking out the two protagonists and asking their souls to take back those remnant pieces of themselves.

"They are happy up there," Cas comes back reporting, holding out the fossil that's undamaged and unhaunted now.

"Guess the movie misses that last bit of the happy ending," Dean comments.

"I don't think everything needs to have a happy ending," Sam answers.

"Hmm," Dean ponders, moving to walk close enough to Cas to brush his arms. *At least for ours, I wish there is.* He thinks.
