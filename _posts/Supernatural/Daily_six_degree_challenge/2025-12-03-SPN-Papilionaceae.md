**Meaning:**

n. leguminous plants whose flowers have butterfly-shaped corollas; commonly included in the family Leguminosae (*from WordNet 3.0 by Princeton University*)

Papilionaceae, or Faboideae, is a subfamily of the flowering plant family Fabaceae (or Leguminosae), widely distributed, members including the pea, the sweet pea, the laburnum, and other legumes. (*from Wikipedia*)

**Scene:**

It took them a whole night to drive from Dick Roman's place to Cas's hospital. Dean pushed Baby to her limit, but still there was a limit, and the road was long. Meg called them almost immediately when they crushed open the tablet, but it took about seventeen calls to wake Dean up from the mysterious tablet coma they got.

They started driving north immediately. And that was eight last night.

Dean walks into the hospital corridor, rubbing his fingers anxiously. The sun is up already. Sunshine casts into the stiff white space, adding a tad little color to the cold, ominous space. But it isn't the hospital's fault for looking like a hospital.

He's scared to shit, not knowing what he'll see, what he'll say. Meg kept it so vague that all they really got from her was, "Cas is awake," but nothing more. The questions haunted Dean all the way when he was driving. Sometimes he was so distracted that Sam had to shout out to drag his attention back to the road. And he would still see Cas's terrified face when he first got the devil insanity transferred into his head.

Cas's room is empty.

Dean checks twice, but the room is still empty. Cas's suit and shirt lie on the top level of the open cabinet at the feet of the bed; the sheet on the bed is rumpled, clearly slept in. All the light bulbs are broken. Neither Cas nor Meg is in the room.

"No," Dean breathes out a single syllable, feeling his chest too tight to breathe through.

*It can't be. It can't be. It can't be.*

He murmurs, dashing out of the ward, leaving Sam in the room.

Empty hospital room means... means...

He can't think about that, he can't. Cas can't be... that, right? They just got him back, he just remembered himself, he just woke up last night, he couldn't possibly...

Dean's fingers clench into a fist, pressing on his chest hard. *He should have stayed here with Cas,* his next thought says. *He should never leave and only ask a demon to look after Cas. And now... too late, it's all too late.* He starts running. He has to get out of here. This whole place reminds him too much of the hospital he lay in after that car crash some long years ago, the hospital where he lost his father.

He runs into someone in a nurse's gown, almost knocking them over.

"Geez, so eager to see me?" An oversweet voice says.

Dean takes a step back.

It's Meg.

"Where's Cas?" Dean says, breathless.

"Outside, in the garden," Meg points a lazy finger toward the end of the corridor. "Don't startle him, by the way. He's... different."

Dean doesn't wait to hear the rest of her sentence before he starts darting toward the door.

The garden is vast and pretty, he has to admit. It must be a regular place for the hospitalized patients to take a brief walk when they can. The flowers he doesn't know the names of are blossoming brightly, with cheerful colors and elegant shapes, stretching onwards and upwards toward the sun. Insects fly among them, bees and something, buzzing and hovering.

The sun starts to warm up.

Dean scans around.

There's only one figure in the garden, kneeling next to a bright purple flower, the petals stretching out like a butterfly ready to set off. His figure is so thin and small when he's just kneeling there, most of his body covered by the loose trench coat. The skirt of the coat drags on the ground.

Dean wants to run toward him, but on second thought, he remembers Meg's reminder. *Don't startle him.*

Dean walks slowly toward him instead. Feeling his heart beat so fast inside his chest that he's probably going to explode.

"Cas?" He calls carefully.

There's still about ten steps between them.

Cas puts a finger to his lips, still staring at the flower, fully concentrated. "Shhh, don't frighten the bee," he says.

Dean is practically tiptoeing now, moving slowly toward the angel, kneeling down beside him as quietly as he can. He turns his head, and Cas is right here, next to him. Dean studies his side profile. He looks different. Just like Meg said, something is different in him. Cas looks... so calm and peaceful, in a way he never was before. The constant furrow between his eyebrows is gone. His eyes are bright and curious, full of joy, like a kid seeing a new creature for the first time, or just seeing anything new and beautiful, in general.

*He looks like a kid now.* Dean thinks. *And an angel.* Like the kind of halo and harp angel the children's book would tell about.

He's still wearing the hospital's white gown underneath his trench coat, Dean notices. And a pair of thin paper slippers, without socks. All just adding more resemblance to a young, innocent child, that's nothing like a million-year-old angel.

"Cas?" Dean lets his voice out as quietly as possible, barely whispering.

"Look," Cas answers, pointing a finger at the flower before him, with wide eyes shining with excitement. There's a bee in between the petals, covered by the big banner petal on top, so Dean doesn't see it at first.

But he still doesn't know what he is supposed to be looking at. He drove a whole night rushing here, to see the angel that hadn't been conscious for days and weeks, and now Cas is just excited to stare at some bees and wild flowers? Something is definitely off.

"What am I looking at?" Dean asks hoarsely.

"Papilionaceae," Cas answers smoothly, "a subfamily of the Fabaceae, or you might know it as Leguminosae. Or some people just simply call it..."

"Cas," Dean has to reach out a hand to stop him. His palm hovers just above Cas's arm, not sure if he's allowed to touch or not. Cas finally turns to look at him. A little confused, a little remnant of the joy and curiosity he saw earlier.

"What are you doing here?" Dean asks him.

Cas tilts his head slightly toward the flower. "Watching the flower," he considers his own answer, then adds. "And the bee. The bee is very important. It has already collected three different flowers this morning while I'm watching. Did I tell you it's called Papilionaceae because its flower looks like a butterfly? You see, *papilion* means butterfly in Latin. Isn't it miraculously beautiful?"

He turns back to regard the flower fondly, only to find the bee has left when he wasn't watching.

"Where did it go?" Cas pouts, standing up, a little upset now that he lost track of his bee.

Dean can't say a word, just quietly falling apart inside. Something is definitely different and perhaps broken there with his angel, but he doesn't know where to start fixing, or if he wants to. It's almost like running into Emmanuel again, before Cas remembered who he is. And now, a kid inside the angel's body, with probably all of his knowledge. But he looks almost happy, relieved, and easy.

Dean knows he would trade anything to not have to live with all the burdens on his shoulders, to just be simple and happy. And now, it looks like Cas just gets exactly that; how can he break the bubble *again* and remind him of all the pain? Can't he let Cas be happy and simple just for a little longer, talking about his flowers, watching his bees?

So Dean doesn't say anything. Doesn't mention the Leviathans, the tablet, the broken arm of a girl named Charlie, or that Dick is probably right on their tail, and the world is probably going to turn into trash, and every human becomes the Leviathans' food probably very soon.

He doesn't mention any of that, not now, not yet. And instead, Dean says, "Yeah, Cas, it's beautiful." Because the flower really is, especially since it's Cas who points it out. And, "Let's find the bee."
