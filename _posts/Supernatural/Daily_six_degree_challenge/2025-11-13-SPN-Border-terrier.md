**Scene:**

"Listen, I think it's a big decision, so we should all get a vote," Sam says seriously, sitting down on the couch next to Eileen, setting his cup of tea down on the little table.

It feels damn like one of those formal family meetings that they always thought only existed on those bad TV drama shows -- it's almost always about someone getting diagnosed with something bad, or someone having a baby, or how to handle a will or some sudden fortune.

Dean is suddenly tense, shifting on Sam's new sofa in his new apartment. "What's the problem?"

Cas puts a hand on his thigh. Dean grabs it automatically.

"Relax, nothing bad," his brother chuckles slightly. "It's about Miracle."

Dean's shoulder relaxes. "What about him?"

"I think it's about time to decide... ugh, his future happiness," Sam says.

Dean frowns at him. "What do you mean?"

"He's old enough now for a castration, or we might need to find him a mate. He's leaving scent all over the house lately." Sam looks around the house, pointedly nodding at the feet of the sofa that Dean and Cas are sitting on.

Dean glances at the same spot, getting his point.

"We're not cutting his balls off!" He raises his voice protectively almost instantly, making a hand gesture in the air high above his thighs. His own face makes a grimacing look just from imagining, "No! Let him be a boy and have all the boy parts."

Sam laughs. "That's not really how it works. But alright, I don't really have a strong opinion either way," he glances at Eileen and Cas, both of whom nod quietly. "That's decided, then. He'll continue to be a man dog, but that means we'll have to find him a lady friend. I looked online, I think he's probably from a terrier origin, but God, there are like a million types of different terriers, can you believe it? There's like border terrier, fox terrier, rat terrier, bull terrier, then there's also American terrier, Australian terrier, Yorkshire terrier, Irish terrier. No way I can narrow it down to his real breed. But there might be some other terrier places we can check out..."

Dean cuts him off, "Hold on a sec. For one, how do you know he's even *into* lady dogs?"

Sam stares at him, ridiculous. Eileen tries so hard to stop a laugh from bursting out that her lips are twitching at the edge.

Dean ignores him, holding a hand to gesture while continuing, the other hand still holding Cas's without himself realizing. Cas doesn't move an inch or try to get his hand back anyway. "Secondly, even if he does, which might be a big if, why does it have to be the same breed? Hell, why does it even have to be the same species? He might be into... I don't know, cats, bees, birds... anything? Am I right?" he turns to Cas, eyes asking for support.

Cas blinks. "Statistically speaking, the likelihood is very low." He licks his lips, glancing down at Dean's, watching the tip of his lips showing a sign of dropping, so he quickly squeezes Dean's hand, holding tighter. "But it's not impossible. And given our track record of running into things that are unlikely, I'd say it might be a high possibility. We should let Miracle choose for himself, see who or what he falls in love with."

"See?" Dean beams brightly, turning back to his brother and sister-in-law. "Free love era. He gets to pick for himself. We'll just need to show him all those creatures and everything out there. No arranged marriage or anything!"

Sam sighs exaggeratedly. "Can you stop projecting yourself onto the freaking dog for one second, Dean?"

"What? No! I'm not projecting anything!" Dean protests loudly, ignoring the fact that his and Cas's fingers are laced now.

"You are totally projecting," Eileen says as she signs, while smiling so widely that even her eyes are glinting. "But I agree. Free love is the best."

So it is settled. Miracle gets to pick the love of his life, with his free will, just like everyone in the room.
