**Scene:**

Dean wakes up in the middle of the night.

Something doesn't feel right, but he refuses to open his eyes just yet. But his subconscious brain is already working. Something doesn't feel right.

He stretches his arms and legs to the other side of the bed. "Cas?" he mumbles.

The other side is empty.

His hand grips a handful of empty covers and blankets. Without body warmth. Without the body that is supposed to be lying inside, snoring adorably, nuzzling him back.

Dean snaps his eyes open, turning on the bedside lamp.

The other side of the bed is truly, completely, absolutely missing a huge, big person.

"Cas?" Dean raises his voice, not realizing his voice is trembling.

He doesn't hear the familiar voice of his husband answering him, but there is some quiet rustling sound outside the bedroom, like a rat chewing something. Or could be a raccoon. Or a thief.

Dean stops his brain from thinking about what could get worse after that.

His hand fumbles underneath his pillow on reflex, but not finding the familiar metal. Then he remembers he moved the pistol to the bedside drawer three months ago, when Cas said he should at least try to get used to not having to startle awake and get ready for fighting. He had to spend the first two months holding Cas's hand to stop his own hand from fumbling under his pillow.

Dean slips off the bed with the pistol, ignoring the fact that he's only wearing shorts. The carpet covers any sound his bare feet might have made, for which Dean appreciates.

The slight crackling sound comes from the kitchen, and no light is on.

Dean holds his breath and approaches gingerly. The moonlight outside the shade-covered window leaves thin strips of light on the floor, but not enough for illumination.

Dean hides at the turn of the kitchen counter, counting to three in his heart, then at once, uses an elbow to snap open the light while his hands click off the safety bolt. "Hands up! Who are you?!" he demands.

A clear, crispy sound of something drops to the floor.

Dean finds his gun pointing right at his missing husband, with a crack with dipped cheese spread stuffed in his mouth like a hamster.

The other victim cracker that must be once in Cas's hand is now crashed on the floor, shattered.

Cas has his hands raised up as demanded, blinking at him guiltily.

Dean bursts out in a huge laugh, putting the safety bolt back on the gun, leaving it on the counter. "Dammit!" he says, rubbing his nose, still haven't stopped laughing.

"What are you doing in the middle of the night? Not on the bed." Dean pouts slightly, stepping into the kitchen, kneeling down to pick up the cracker.

Cas puts down his hands slowly, chewing the one in his mouth. The spread leaves a tiny yellow dip on the corner of his mouth without him realizing.

"I felt hungry," Cas answers sheepishly. "I didn't think you'd wake up and find out."

Dean cleans up the bits and crumbs, rinses his hands, then turns to him, raising his eyebrows with amusement. "Why do I get the feeling this isn't the first time?"

Cas ducks his head, grinning slightly. "It's not. Remember when we were still living in the bunker and Jack was living with us?"

"Yeah?"

"Did you ever wonder why the breakfast cereal always runs out so fast?" Cas glances at Dean.

Dean widens his eyes. "Holy, Sammy *always* blames *me* for that. So it's you two taking a midnight snack!"

Cas nods with a small grin. Then, "Mostly Jack, but sometimes I do, too. Just to have something to do at night."

"That delicious?"

"I wouldn't know. But definitely not as good as these." Cas points at the snacks on the counter.

Dean leans in to kiss the remnant of the cheese spread off Cas's lips. A small sweet peck. Cas hums happily and kisses back.

Dean licks his lips after pulling back, satisfied. "I'd say it's pretty good." He chuckles. "That explains it. But I still need to decide if I can forgive someone for waking me up and making me think I'm abandoned by my husband in the middle of the night." He pretends to pout, glancing at Cas with a small grin.

Cas frowns slightly, holding out to wrap his arms around Dean's waist. "Sorry," he murmurs quietly. "Didn't mean to make you worry." He pulls Dean closer to him, feeling his heartbeat calming down against his chest. He kisses the side of Dean's cheek.

"Bed?"

"Yeah."
