**Meaning:**

adj. Journalists use **dovish** to describe politicians or governments who are in favor of using peaceful and diplomatic methods to achieve something, rather than using force and violence. (*from Collins Dictionary*)

Like a dove; innocent. (*from The Century Dictionary*)

**Scene:**

Cas squints at the screen, studying it like it's the most difficult case.

Dean glances at him in the middle of a slow scene, holding out the popcorn bowl in front of him.

Cas picks one mindlessly, but isn't eager to eat it. His eyes are still fixated on the screen.

"Let me know if you need some explanation or something," Dean offers, watching the characters start walking around the castle on the screen again. He isn't actually sure if he gets all the story there, but it's always fun to watch Cas watch something and ask all kinds of questions. But perhaps *Game of Thrones* is still too difficult an option for now.

"This is very complicated," Cas murmurs, staring as if he can see through those people's heads and understand their thoughts just like that.

"Which part?" Dean debates whether he should pause it, but he ends up still leaving that episode on.

Cas makes a vague gesture that's practically saying "everything".

But he says, "Let me try to understand it by myself first. After all, I want to be able to understand humans."

So Dean lets him.

They take a short break after the episode, and Dean takes a quick bathroom run.

Cas is scrolling through his smartphone with an even deeper frown.

Dean deliberately sits right next to him, squeezing their legs together, and wiping his still-damp hand on Cas's pants, who completely doesn't notice.

"What are you reading?" Dean extends his neck to read.

Cas turns the screen to him. The phone browser has a page open: "One article to explain the Greens and the Blacks."

"Ah, did it talk about all the politics?" Dean sinks back into the couch, holding the popcorn bowl back into his cuddle, folding his legs onto the seat comfortably.

"Yes," Cas puts down his phone with a sigh. "But it seems it's complicating things even more. It starts talking about different types of political attitude, something about hawkish and dovish. I don't understand what hawks and doves have to do with everything. I can only see dragons, wolves, and some other creatures on the flags."

Dean chuckles quietly, holding out to share his popcorn again, just to let Cas's fingers reach in and brush his hand unconsciously. Cas takes a second one, playing with it between his fingers, then puts it carefully between his teeth, as if he's analyzing it as well during his contemplation.

"I'm no expert in that, too." Dean shrugs easily. "If it makes you feel better, half the time, I can't get what those tricky mind games those people are playing." He hints at the TV screen with his chin, popping another piece of popcorn into his mouth. "All I know is this: when I think about dovish, I think about something peaceful, anti-war, very diplomatic; and hawkish is just the opposite. But yeah, you'll have to ask Sammy to get all the fancy terms and a real walk-through on the episodes. Be careful, though, he might hate you if you spoil the ending of this season to him," Dean chuckles again. "I know he didn't have time to catch up yet."

Cas nods thoughtfully.

Dean nudges him playfully. "You're still frowning."

"Does that mean I'm hawkish, too? Just like Michael, Lucifer, and Raphael?" Cas says slowly. "It seems only Gabriel can be called dovish."

Dean unfolds his legs, sitting straight slowly. He puts the popcorn down, landing a hand on Cas's shoulder. "Hey, you alright? What's that about? You're nothing like those archangel assholes."

Cas turns to him, eyes sad. "The angel civil war," he answers glumly. "I was just as aggressive, bloodthirsty, and belligerent as all of them. When I was bad. I was even worse. And there was no denial or argument you can say it's not. Because I know exactly what I was doing and I did them." He bites his lips, looking down. Pained.

"Hey, hey," Dean pulls him into a hug, pressing his palms on the angel's back, shushing him quietly. It's not the first time Cas got dragged back into the old events, but every time, it still pains. It still pains both of them. The past still haunts them. Dean didn't know what to do the first time around, and honestly, he still doesn't quite know what's the best thing to do, but at least he learns how to make Cas feel just a little better and get over the episode.

"You're forgiven," Dean whispers into his ear, pressing their cheeks together. He can feel Cas breathe heavily, leaning his chin on Dean's shoulder. Dean holds him tighter.

"It's okay now," Dean tells him quietly.

"It's okay now."

"Everything is okay now."

He tells the angel again and again.

Cas stays in his arms for a long time.
