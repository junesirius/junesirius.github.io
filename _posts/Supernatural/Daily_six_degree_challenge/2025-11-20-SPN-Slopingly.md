**Scene:**

For the record, Dean wants to note, for anyone who's willing to listen -- this is all Cas's fault.

Cas just *had to* insist on doing *everything* the human way, when he could just flip a finger and get it done in the blink of an eye. Then none of the other things would ever happen.

"But I want to do it slowly, like you do." Cas insisted, looking at him so innocently with his blue, blue eyes.

Dean had to agree.

So there they are, building their house together, plank by plank, nail by nail.

It's not that Dean didn't enjoy. He's thrilled, actually, more than happy to spend all the time in the world with Cas, building their own place together. They can't always live in the bunker after all. Sam is more than happy to move out with Eileen, too.

And it's getting closer to Christmas. Dean really wants the place done before the first snow hits, so he can invite his brother and sister-in-law to come over. He's imagining a little Christmas tree and a classic old movie night together with his whole family. So maybe -- maybe he's getting a little impatient. But this is definitely not his fault.

<br>

"That roof is definitely not flat," Dean yells from the root of the ladder, looking up. His neck is getting sore from crooking up for the whole afternoon. His back starts aching, too. He hates how sometimes his body just wants to remind him he's not on the right side of the twenties anymore.

"It is parallel to the sea surface," Cas insists, nailing the plank into the position, providing one more slice of shade.

This is the last piece of the puzzle. The garage. No way Dean is gonna make it a sloppy job for Baby's room.

"We're miles from the sea! How could anyone possibly know it's like sea level flat?!" Dean raises his voice, his temper bubbling. "It has an angle to the ground! Everyone would just assume it's the roof done wrong and laugh at us!"

"But you said you want the roof 'horizontal'! Well, that's the horizontal!" Cas lets go of his hands on the side of the ladder, waving back at Dean to give him the air quote, annoyed. "I can't make it slopingly but say it's flat! It's just factually incorrect! You didn't say it needs to be 'parallel to the ground'!"

"How am I supposed to know the ground isn't 'parallel to the sea'?" Dean glares back at him, removing his hands from holding the ladder, mocking Cas in a similar air quote. "I'm just a human! I can't freaking see what the sea level looks like. I'm not like you!"

"Oh, so it's my fault being an angel now?" Cas snaps, squinting his eyes. "Is that what you're saying?"

"I didn't say that!" Dean wipes a hand over his face, frustrated. "For Christ's sake, why is this so freaking difficult?"

He checks his watch, then. "You know what, I need to go get a drink. Don't wait for me for dinner."

Dean slips out in the Impala without waiting for an answer. He feels like shit.

<br>

The bar doesn't provide any comfort. And beer isn't strong enough to wipe his mind.

He switches to something stronger after the first few glasses.

Dean can feel his phone vibrating in his pocket, right against his thigh. But he decidedly doesn't check it.

He doesn't leave until the bar is about to close.

He almost crushes the car into the new fence around the garden that they spent two full days getting done. Cas would kill him if he rolled over his vegetable and knocked down that bee-shaped pot -- Dean picked it for him at some random store, and that became Cas's favorite thing of everything.

Dean's face softens. He parks the car in the new garage. The ladder has been put back into the corner. The rest of the planks are finished. But Dean's foggy, swimming mind can't tell if the roof is flat or not.

He staggers into the bedroom.

The room is empty.

He blinks a few times, the drunkenness leaving him fast as his blood starts getting cold. He turns on the light. The bed is really empty.

Cas isn't in the living room, either. Or kitchen. Or any room.

Dean turns on all the lights in the house, standing in the middle of the suddenly too vast, too empty living room. Lost.

"Cas?" he tries, his voice coming out broken.

No one answers.

He blinks again, feeling his eyes sting.

The room echoes his voice faintly.

*No.*

Dean's breath shatters.

The night is too deep, too quiet. As if there is no second person existing in the world.

Like that day in his worst nightmare.

Dean leans against a wall, slumping down slowly. He buries his face in his palms.

*What had he just done?*

<br>

His phone rings in his pocket, startling Dean out of his personal Hell.

His fingers fumble frantically on the screen when he sees Cas's name on it.

"Which bar are you in?" Cas's voice comes through, worried. "You shouldn't be driving. Sorry, I was too pissed, I forgot. I'm picking you up."

"Cas," Dean's voice shakes.

"Are you okay, Dean?" Cas rushes eagerly. "Where are you?"

"Home," he chokes the words out through his tightened throat. "I'm home."

"I'll be there immediately."

The sound of wings fluttering is on the phone as well as right before him. Cas is still holding the phone to his ear when he appears before Dean.

Dean wipes his face hard, looking up. "I'm sorry," he blurts out rapidly. "I'm so sorry, Cas. Forget about that stupid roof, you can do whatever you like." *As long as you are still here.*

"Hey, hey," Cas kneels down before him, shaking his shoulders slightly, dragging him into a hug. Dean clings to his back like he's drowning.

"Don't worry about the roof," Cas's cheek rubs against Dean's. He tells him gently. "I got it fixed. The way you like it. The sloping-flat way. I was just out looking for you." *Don't worry. I will not leave you.*

Dean rests his chin on Cas's shoulder, his breath easing down.

"Slat," he says, out of nowhere.

"What?" Cas pulls back slightly, checking him up.

Dean lets out a teary chuckle. "Sloping-flat, that makes it *slat*. Or do you like floping better?"

Cas chuckles with him. "Whichever you like."
