**Meaning:**

n. (or *catharsis*) ① *a*: purification or purgation of the emotions (such as pity and fear) primarily through art; *b*: a purification or purgation that brings about spiritual renewal or release from tension; ② elimination of a complex by bringing it to consciousness and affording it expression (*from Merriam-Webster Dictionary*)

Katharsis (or *Catharsis*) is getting rid of unhappy memories or strong emotions such as anger or sadness by expressing them in some way (*from Collins Dictionary*)

**Scene:**

The sharp pain on his back starts turning dull, then numbed, then nothing.

Dean Winchester leaves his body.

"It is my honor to see you one last time," Death greets him like an old friend, leading the way.

"What's gonna happen next?" Dean asks.

"You will have to find out yourself," the old Death answers kindly. "I can only escort you to the door. You will be greeted and led by the host of Heaven from there."

"Angels?" Dean jerks his head up sharply. "Who?!" *The angel? The only angel he really desperately wants -- needs -- to see again?*

Death just gestures skyward vaguely. "No spoilers. You will find out soon enough."

<br>

The door Death leads him to is a door decorated by beads curtains. Thin fragrance and some music that sounds like Asian temple music seeps out through the door.

Dean's heart beats fast as he walks closer to the door. It looks too close to that one time Zachariah sent him five years ahead of time that he hasn't really been thinking about for about a decade now. But the door, the hut, the smell and music, it all looks too close.

He opens the bead curtain slowly, edging inside hesitantly.

Cas from his memory of that time sits on a futon on the floor, alone. A delicate censer sits before him, letting out thin white smoke. Dean assumes the fragrance comes from that. He can't take his eyes off the sitting man. He can't even speak, or walk.

The Cas from his memory -- this Cas that sits on a futon with folded legs -- is just too different from the Cas he's familiar with. His eyes are closed, his hands rest on his knees. He still looks vaguely like that master love guru Dean called him back them, but quieter, cleaner, and more whole. Dean doesn't know why his mind supplies him that word, but "whole" is the word that pops into his head.

But he still knows well enough that he's not the Cas he knows. Not the one he lost.

Dean's breath hitches at the thought. Pain crawls back into his heart, threatening to tear him apart.

"You need to come here and sit down," Cas opens his eyes, turning to him, gesturing to the futon that appears before him, opposite side to the censer.

"Cas?" Dean whispers, his lips trembling, moving into the room slowly. His steps too heavy.

The room is warm and comforting.

Cas smiles at him faintly. "Yes, in a way," he answers. "I'm a Heaven's host that manifested from your memory, preparing you for Heaven before actually leading you there. I don't choose the form I'm taking, you are -- your subconsciousness, I mean." He hastily adds the last part of the sentence when Dean opens his mouth, just about to say he didn't choose this -- him, his form from that time. His heart aches, just looking at Cas like this. The most human time he's ever seen Cas, even worse than that time Cas was actually human and working in a gas station shop.

Dean swallows difficultly. "How?" he asks quietly. Too many questions congests his thoughts. *Why are you here? How did my mind choose this version of you? You're just a manifest, then where is the real Cas? How can I get him back?*

"This will be your katharsis," Cas speaks softly, reaching out his hands to gesture the futon again. Dean sits down obediently, folding his legs just like Cas does -- it's not a very comfortable posture, but he does it anyway. He still barely blinks, staring at Cas too hard.

The manifestation is real enough that he can see Cas's eyelashes flapping when he blinks, he can see Cas's lips chapped and dry like they always are, his shirt -- Cas is wearing a comfortable, home shirt, loosely hanging over his thin shoulders. His hairs are not as rumpled and messy as he remembers for his best friend, but exactly like the one he remembers from back then in the Croatoan world -- the world he didn't save, the world where that future version of him and Cas and so many others he didn't save, like so much other worlds that he messed up.

He never gets it right. Dean realizes. He messes up practically every single version of Cas in all the worlds, and eventually, he lost his real, best friend that he doesn't know all those feelings he has about him for one last time. His minds drift so far away that he almost misses what the Cas before him is telling him.

"You have too much thoughts and feelings and emotions, Dean," Cas moves the censer closer to him, catching his attention. The white smoke melts into the air. The burning smell brings Dean's frantically speeding-up heartbeat slow down a little, his focus back to the present. "I think the reason why you're seeing me now, is I'm one of your many guilt -- but I'm probably not even the biggest of them all."

Dean swallows hard again, can't breathe. Cas is right. Cas is always right about him, whichever version of him, whichever world he's in, it's like Cas can always see through him. Cas always knows him better than himself. Even at the very last -- especially at the very last. *You're the most loving man I will ever know. Then you have to know I love you just as much as you love me. You have to, do you?* Dean thinks bitterly, too painful to bear the thoughts. He thought about every single day after Cas is gone, he doesn't realize he'd think about that every single day again when he reaches the end of his mortal life, when he's in Heaven. *Is this gonna be how he spend the eternity up here? Painful and agonizing about all the past and experiencing all those memory forever?*

"You would not," Cas answers him, hearing his thoughts. Dean is too tired to ask why or how he can do that -- it doesn't surprise him anymore what Cas (or anything remotely like Cas) can do. "What we will do here," he nods at the room in general, "during this process, we will go through your emotions and feelings. The katharsis will purge your anger, your guilt, your fear, or even your grief. At the end of is, you will be calm and peaceful, only then you're ready to move on to the real Heaven, your final home."

Dean stands up so quickly that the motion makes his head dizzy. "No!" he yells at Cas -- the manifestation of Cas that Heaven sent him, the wrong one, the fake one, he reminds himself, *This is not Cas. His Cas is somewhere else.* "No!" Dean yells again, so loud that his voice echoes in the small space. He takes a step back, away from this Cas. "You can shove the calm and peace up your lily white ass! I'll keep my anger, my guilt, even my grief! Especially my grief! I'm not gonna just give up my feelings and everything just to lie back down in Heaven when I know Cas is not there! Even it means I'm gonna suffer for eternity, 'cause he's in the Empty, alone, dark, and suffering! That's not gonna happen! You hear me? Never!" Rage burns through his mind and runs through his veins. He's outrageous.

The Cas manifested by Heaven sighs heavily, standing up slowly from the futon, stretching his arms in a way that looks far too soft and real, as if he's really from that alternate timeline back then, still vaguely stoned but clear enough to know what he's doing. "I should know," he says easily, not bothered by Dean's sudden temper. "Dean Winchester. Of course you will never give me up. What do you want me to do, then?"
