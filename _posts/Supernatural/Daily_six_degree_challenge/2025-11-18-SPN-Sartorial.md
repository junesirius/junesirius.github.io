**Scene:**

"Dear Mr. and Mr. Winchester, it is with great pleasure that we invite you to join us for an evening of joy and dancing at Longbourn House next Thursday night at eight o'clock. Your attendance will be of great honor -- bla, bla," Dean reads the parchment letter that the mail boy just put in his hand, eyebrows raised high in amusement.

"Holy shit, Cas," he turns to the angel, still holding the paper, still too amused to keep the tips of his lips down. "How did you even do that? We're here for like ten minutes, and you already got us the big ball invitation?"

"Technically, we are already here for two days," Cas corrects him. "And I merely chatted with the lovely lady in the market, who happened to be the youngest daughter of the Bennet family."

"And you told her where we live and our real names."

"I..." Cas licks his lips. A motion he just picked recently. "I don't believe it would do any harm," he mumbles quietly, shifting a little.

"Did you tell her we're married and it's your idea to come here for a honeymoon?" Dean chuckles slightly at his little move, reaching out a hand to stop the angel's fingers from fidgeting, squeezing gently.

Cas hesitates, glancing at Dean's look, "Do you wish I did?"

Dean shrugs. "I don't really mind either way. Technically, we aren't married until two hundred years later -- time travel, always makes things complicated."

Cas agrees, then shifting his attention back to the invitation. "The dancing. You coming?"

"You kidding me? Can't let my husband get all the dances with others and not be there with him, can I?" Dean laughs.

"I thought you would say you don't know how to dance." Cas relaxes.

"I don't, but I'm sure we can figure that out. But before that, shouldn't we figure out something to wear? This," he gestures at his shirt and jacket, "can't possibly work. I look like an alien, honestly."

They go into the town for the tailor's.

Dean bites his tongue to stifle the chuckle when the lady in the shop takes his measure. But he raises his eyebrows when she does the same for Cas. The measure includes, well, all the sizes in all the places on their body -- just to make sure the cloth will fit the most perfectly, of course. Watching those nimble hands and fingers stroking through the angel's body, Dean doesn't know if he's feeling jealous or just feeling like jumping on him right now and taking apart those existing clothes on him. Maybe both.

Cas warns him with a look and a glance around the shop. *Not now.* His eyes are saying. Dean sighs quietly. Of course Cas knows what filthy thing he's thinking; they didn't date for fifteen years for nothing.

"They will be finished and delivered to your address by next week," the lady tells them as she finishes.

<br>

The clothes are astounding. Even more so when they are on them. But God, they're complicated.

"Now I know why those princesses in the history dramas need like a dozen servants to put those dresses on. You do need like twenty hands to figure this thing out -- God, how does it even work?" Dean grumbles, trying to push his arm over something that resembles a sleeve.

Cas picks the piece up and turns it around, settling it around his neck quietly, smoothening it below the dress shirt's collar. "Here," he says gently, pressing it flat.

"Or they can have one angel that knows everything to make it work." Dean leans in, giving the angel a brief kiss on the lip as appreciation. "Thanks."

<br>

All the complications in the dressing and preparation, as it turns out, work out amazingly.

The one for Cas tightens around his waist, extending long behind the tail, like a beautiful, elegant bird's tail. The white shirt underneath has layers and layers of white collars that look like a flower bouquet before his chest. Dean can't really get used to the similar look on his own, claiming it just looks too weird and unlike him, but Cas insists he looks absolutely beautiful and glamorous. Which turns into a snuggly session before the mirror that makes them almost late for the ball. (But no one needs to know. They fixed the broken collars before they actually head out, anyway.)

The manor is vast. Dean glances at the chandeliers and decorations, and can't say he isn't impressed.

"Man, rich people," he whispers to Cas under his breath, walking into the ballroom together.

They're drowned by a sea of -- no, a whole freaking ocean of -- beauties. The cute, young ladies in fancy, floral dresses; gentlemen in dresses Dean doesn't really know the names for, but man, every single person in the room looks unbelievably amazing.

"This is just way too *Pride and Prejudice*," Dean tilts his head, leaning slightly to talk to Cas.

"I didn't realize you read the book," Cas replies, then quickly adds, "I didn't mean it like, you don't read, just --"

"I didn't." Dean chuckles. "But I watched like three or four adaptations. They do know how to find the most gorgeous man to play Mr. Darcy, you know?"

Cas hums noncommittally.

A man walks slowly in their direction when the music starts.

Dean's eyes land on his curly black hair and sharp features. He nudges the angel, whispering, "I can't believe it, he's just like Colin Firth!"

The man stops before them before Cas can answer.

"Greetings, gentlemen," he speaks in a pure, elegant English accent. "Your sartorial splendor is truly marvelous, as is your glamor."

He turns to Cas completely then, making a small bow with his upper body. "May I have the honor of the next dance?" He reaches out a hand.

Dean swallows.

Cas answers easily, "I'm afraid you'll have to ask for my partner's permission first." He breaks the look with the man, turning to Dean with a small smile.

The man is surprised, but only for a brief second. "My apologies." his eyes are brown, genuine and kind, turning to Dean. "You're a lucky, as well as astonishing, man yourself."

"I know," Dean blurts out with a grin he can't hold back.

"May I?" the man makes a polite gesture at Cas again, smiling humbly, showing a small dimple on his cheek.

Dean pretends to hesitate. "Alright. As long as he's mine for the rest of the night."

"You know I already am, and always will be." Cas smiles to him, promising.
