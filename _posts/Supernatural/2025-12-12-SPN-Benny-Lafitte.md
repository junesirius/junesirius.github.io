You have a manipulative father figure who considers himself a god and controls your life. You and others who are like you work for him for years, until you find the love of your life and decide to leave the old life behind to live a life of your own. Your father figure takes away the one you're in love with and sends you to a horrifyingly awful place. You save a man there, and with your help, the two of you escape the place together. He helps you avenge, and the two of you are so close that you are like brothers, or found family, or even closer, but neither one of you really talks about it. His own younger brother doesn't like you, and the two of them have big fights because of you. You are alone and have no friends, completely desolated, lonely, depressed, feel you don't belong, and all you have is this one friend, and all you want is to call him and have a coffee together to make you feel better. But he tells you no, because of his brother, he can't be there for you, he tells you this is the end for the two of you. He's breaking up with you, as a friend, and you understand because his brother always comes first for him. And then, his brother is in trouble, and he tells you he has to kill you to save his brother. And you think, you don't belong anyway, you don't have anyone besides him, you can do anything to help him even if it means your death, so you say it's okay, you look into his eyes, smile a little, teary-eyed a little, and you're killed by his own hands, die in his arms.

This is the story of Benny Lafitte.

Why does it sound so familiar?
