## Claim

### Claim Summary

The story is S12E23 canon divergent. Written in two halves, each is like a tape side (the title is a song name), first half Crowley POV, second half Dean POV.

For Crowley POV part, the story begins with him sacrificing himself to close the rift (S12E23 canon), and as he dies, his life flashes before his eyes. So flashback memories of his whole life, but emphasize on S09E23-S10E02 time when Dean was a demon and the two of them are having a lot of fun. Crowley sees Dean's demon true form; he teaches Dean how to fly with demon wings; they wear cowboy hats together and take photos. Crowley gets human blood drunk and confesses his feelings for Dean, and that "he deserves to be loved" (like he did in S08E23), and Dean doesn't have morality then, so he and demon!Dean are chaotically casually together. Crowley really wants Dean to rule Hell with him but Dean doesn't want, the two split, Dean ruins the deal by killing the douche client which annoys Crowley, the rest of S10-S12 are fast-forwarded and mostly canon-compliant, but Crowley is very much still highly emotional and has pining feelings for Dean, but doesn't believe he'd ever be important enough in Dean's heart. So come back to reality, he kills himself, believing even his death wouldn't affect Dean much.

(In this divergent version, Crowley closes the rift quickly and early enough so Cas is not dead, Mary is not trapped, Lucifer doesn't come back, so the only bad thing after the event is Crowley's death.)

Switch to second half Dean POV, Dean is shocked by Crowley's suicidal self-sacrifice, recalling their past. So similar to the first half, a lot of flashback memories from Dean POV. Dean actually does have some soft feeling for Crowley, especially during his demon time, he saw Crowley's true form for the first time, the two did have a really good time together. But he buried the feeling, believing it's only because he was a demon back then, so naturally attracted by another demon, nothing deep. Fast-forward the rest of their history, come back to the current time, everyone else considers it a victory, only Dean realizes he's quietly mourning Crowley. Others don't think it's worth risking to bring Crowley back, but Dean is (unconsciously) doing that. Hell doesn't have a King after Crowley's death, so Dean becomes Hell's new King, sitting on Crowley's throne, still mourning him. So Crowley's wish for him to rule Hell comes true after all, but Crowley can't see it.

The story ends with a hopeful ending, Crowley is woken by Dean's strong longing in the Empty (similar to Cas at S13 beginning), hinting that he is likely to come back.

Dean sits on Crowley's throne chair, drinking beer, looking at the photo of the two of them wearing cowboy hats and laughing (the photo was taken when Dean was a demon).

And if I'm lucky, I mean, really lucky, if my bleeding brain allows me, I might even get to relive some of the best moments; fingers crossed -- I'll take what I can, since I can't have a nice bottle of a-hundred-year-old Craig right this moment. Here we go."

<br>

Crowley staggers a step back, his hand still holding tight on the Angel Blade in his hand, buried deep in his stomach. He gives it another push, feeling the hot burning sensation spreading across his meatsuit like a flash, reaching his brain like a wild fire. It's fast, but the brain works faster. He'll have one second, maybe two.

Crowley sinks inside, falling into the world of his past.

Lucifer's gloating smile is still lingering on his face, but even he can sense something is not right. "What are you smiling at?" He narrows his eyes, for that smile that's blossoming on the petty demon's face is too bright even for him.

Crowley does not answer. He doesn't hear. He's not looking at Lucifer anymore, his eyes unfocused, not seeing anything, but also seeing everything never more clearly.

<br>

Crowley remembers the first time when the world seems to brighten before his eyes. Everything so much sharper, so much more vivid.

Not his birth, of course -- why would he want to remember those short sixty-year of his pathetic mortal life anyway, not the birth, not the death. Also not his rebirth as a demon, because, well, he doesn't know what he doesn't have access to only after the re-rebirth.

The human blood Moose pumps into his veins burn like lava. It's funny, really, to think the Winchester boys always talk about blood and family and all, and Crowley would never have thought for another millions of years that one day, real-deal Winchester blood would be pumping in his very own flesh-and-blood body. Not his body, per say, but close enough.

Crowley wants to laugh, wants to mock, wants to tell Sam how impossible it is, whatever they want to do. But Crowley finds his brain lagging in that chain of thought -- the chain that he used to keep as sharp and bright as the best chains in his chamber inside his palace. But now, he's already forgetting why he should or shouldn't laugh.

Now, he feels sad. Huge wave of sadness throws him into the sea of bitterness. His head is heating up, he thinks he's burning, like the way whenever a demon burns to death, that's how he'd imagine that would feel like. Extremely heated and sweaty, and then, poof. But no, this is different. Crowley tries to focus, but the thoughts inside his head are so crowded, so loud, suddenly. His head is burning up, because there are too many things in there.

Even the crappy old movies and shows that he watched just to pass time are catching up, replaying in his head, speaking loudly. He feels... feelings. So unbelievably many of them, he doesn't even know their names. He doesn't even know if they ever have a name.

Crowley pants hard, shaking his head, as if a drowning man trying desperately to reach to the surface of the sea that he's thrown into. But his eyes are still fussy, only the gigantic figure of a Winchester is in view. The only *human*, in this world, that's right here, with him.

"Sam," Crowley starts slowly, feeling his throat as dry as the Sahara and not in the fun way. He feels his eyes are burning too, red, damp, teary. Bloody Hell, he thinks he might be crying. But Crowley has become a demon for way too long to remember what *crying* is. He's been a demon for too long, he thinks, probably way too long to take a u-turn and come back good now. But maybe, maybe the Winchesters can understand, maybe, with the tiniest bit of chance, he can still be forgiven, by one of them, or maybe two. "Earlier, when you were confessing back there... what did you say? I only ask because, given my history... it raises the question... Where do I start... to even look for forgiveness? I mean..."

He never gets the answer. Not really. Sam Winchester isn't there to forgive him, and his blunt brother isn't there, not yet.

When Dean charges in, Crowley's vision has already grown so fuzzy and dark that it takes him a full minute to realize what's happening. The boys are crying and all, and he's just there, floating in his own world, thinking, maybe, this is the end of his demon ass. Maybe Dean is there to kill him -- after all, it wouldn't be the first time he tries, and certainly wouldn't be the last. How many years have they known each other back then? The Winchesters have made it very clear every single bloody time that they're only allowing Crowley to live because he's useful. And Crowley thinks, well, he deserves it anyway -- he's a demon after all.

He doesn't expect to survive, and certainly doesn't expect the feeling thing has turned a dive and made his narrowly survived life a real Hell again. Not the locked-in-the-Winchester's-sex-dungeon part, oh no, that's actually the easy part, the nice part of the story. It's the desire and longing and everything pumping and throbbing in his head and inside his chest, they are the worst.

He isn't a stranger to the feeling of being addicted, and drunk, and high, and all of them together at the same time. But the human blood for him, that's the worst, even for a King of Hell. What's worse, the blood syringes that he manages to steal from the Winchesters, this time, as it turns out, they're from Dean.

The minute Dean's blood blends into his veins, Crowley thinks he's on cloud, in his personal Heaven. Just a pump, and Dean's blood races his heart like it will explode right then and there. Crowley yells in pain and high, thinking frantically there is nothing else in the whole world that he'd rather want more than this idiot hunter.

But then, an hour or two later, the real Dean Winchester would come down the stairs to the dungeon, standing before him, arms crossed, with his dirty flannel shirt and patronizing smile, demanding something, a spell, a demon's name, a translation, something.

Crowley glares at him, forcing his head to clear, to forget about the Dean in his head, but look at the Dean before him -- the human, the hunter, *his enemy* (well, as it usually ends up becoming, anyway). He should hate him, Crowley thinks, he and his brother are the reason that he's stuck in this tiny little rathole dungeon, chained to a chair, drugged on human blood, leaving his kingdom to rot. He should spit on him, should kill him when he gets the chance. He thinks, he really thinks that.

And then Dean would start talking. Some spell is probably for some case, some name is to find the leads, some translation of a dead language in some ancient books that probably Crowley is the only living soul that can read (he stubbornly refuses to think about the fact that he doesn't have a soul -- what else do you call that bloody thinking beating and hurting and running out of control inside you then?). Those bloody amber-green eyes stare into his. "Are you helping or not?" Dean would ask.

Crowley only wants to exchange those tiny little pieces of information for some leg-stretching. That's all he can offer, and that's all he's asking for -- that's just how deals work, Crowley's been working with the rule of equal exchange for a thousand years, it's been working just as he loves it to be, but right now, thinking about what pathetic little value he's exchanging for the equally pathetic little benefit he tries to get, it's just... pathetic.

Dean Winchester stops taking the deal. As he should have learned by now.

Crowley still gives him what he wants, anything and everything, and more than what he can offer. And every time when Dean leaves, with the name, with the spell, with the information, Crowley stares at the shadow of his back as he ascends until he disappears behind the closing dungeon door, Crowley thinks to himself, *This is the last time I indulge those two little brats. Dean Winchester, I swear to... whoever's listening, I'm not doing this again.*

And then, it happens again.

Crowley doesn't quite remember many of the things after that, he knows it's something something about Sam dying, something something about Abaddon killing everyone including himself -- the whore must die, of course, but who's the one to pay the price this time isn't a question that Crowley is used to ponder. He's more of a man -- demon -- who shoots first, asks the questions later, just like the Winchesters, which makes it very strange, actually, as to why they'd never seem to quite enjoy his way of doing things when they are just doing it exactly the same way. Crowley never could figure that part out.

But then, he does regret -- somewhat -- about the Mark of Cain. As he tells Dean later, that really is an honest mistake. He doesn't know Mark would have such huge affect on a human, and he certainly doesn't expect the rumor to be true, that the dead hunter would come back a demon.

He wouldn't say he's not thrilled, really.

Watching Dean Winchester's corpse lying breathlessly on his bed makes his heart twitch weirdly inside his chest. Crowley blames the remnant of human blood -- especially Dean's blood -- that must still be there. He's feeling something, but he doesn't quite want to think what that is. It will pass, in the end, like all the after-effect of any drug.

So right now, he just sits down next to Dean's bed, watching the man's mortal bloody face. He never gets to watch Dean in such short distance when he's so quiet before, Crowley realizes. He never gets to sneak close to the Winchesters without being threatened to death after all.

"Dean Winchester," Crowley exhales a long sigh, letting the name linger on tip of his tongue like the best after-taste of a fabulous Scotch. He half thinks the name alone would make his tongue tickle, but the only affect is the voice makes his heart flutter. He can feel adrenaline pumping in his meatsuit, already hypered and -- no, definitely not anxious -- at the thought that Dean would be demon.

He finds himself wondering what color of eyes Dean would have -- black is just for the average, he knows his own is red but that's only for one and only himself the King, Lilith is a bitch who happened to be Lucifer's first so she gets to take a special white, and Dean's probably going to hate the whole world if he comes back with yellow eyes since it's the yellow-eye who killed his mother. *Green would be a nice special color,* Crowley decides. *The one-and-only green-eye demon, just fits so well with his red-eye, they can rule the Hell together.*

He laughs at himself, then. *You're like a nervous new daddy waiting for the baby, guessing the eye colors,* he mocks himself, *Grow up, Dean's not yours.*

But, is it? He means, can it?

He sees the changes before Dean opens his eyes. Black-eyes (or whatever-colored-eyes) isn't the only thing demons have, and only another demon can see them -- their true faces inside the vessel, their wings, their tales, the whole set.

As soon as he puts the First Blade in the dead Dean Winchester's hands, he sees the life pouring back into that cold body. But not like a demon wearing the body like a glove, Dean is inside his own body, and Crowley can just see how his true form grows into existence. His human heart starts beating, but the power is not anything mortal, but the demon that's growing to be. It's like watching a baby developing inside a womb, but much faster, much more excitedly twisted, much more demonic -- and that's a compliment.

The new demon that's going to be the new Dean grows, wearing a face so close to his human face yet still so different. Twisted and darkened, but still so much more beautiful than most -- all -- the demons, besides yours truly, of course. Horns stretch out from his skull in the demonic plane -- which is exactly the reason why humans can never see them -- twisted behind his messy cowboy hair. *If Dean ever sees it, he'd never wear a hat again. Or touch my head.* Then he realizes Dean never touches his head ever anyway.

There are wings, too, the coolest wings in the world -- certainly cooler than the flying ass monkeys like Castiel and those others have, those fluffy birdy wings, so soft and easy to burn and break -- Crowley watches Dean's wings grow and expand with fascination. Demon wings are long, sharp skeletons covered by hard solid membranes, working perfectly like the best shield ever -- they don't have the habit of lighting a light show and showcase their wings like how angels enjoy to do, as if it's their naughty bits, but that doesn't mean they don't have cool, awesome wings. God must have created dragons and bats after demons. He can already imagine what Dean would say when he finally can see his and feel them.

Dean never asked how demons can fly, though, Crowley realizes. It must just be natural then, angels have wings and can fly -- though humans seem to love to call it "teleport", and Dean must already figured out that, since demons can also fly -- or teleport, whatever -- demons must also have wings. It's still going to be so different to actually see them versus only knowing them.

Crowley's wings twitch at the thought, itching to spread and flap and take him somewhere to fly around, but Crowley clenches his hands to order his wings to settle down (okay so it seems there really is some reason that wings *can* be called naughty bits). He has to be here when Dean Winchester opens his eyes. He *has to* be the first one the new-born sees. It just has to be him.

He can't really see Dean's wings and tails too well when his human body is still laying flat on his bed, flattening his demon bits in the demonic plane. Crowley can feels all the curiosity and pumping and beating inside his chest, scratching him like nasty claws.

"Listen to me, Dean Winchester," Crowley says, but there are so many he wants to teach him, to show him, to let him see what he sees, where does he even start? "What you're feeling right now -- it's not death. It's life -- a new kind of life."

He watches Dean's wings spread longer, reaching farther, though still just instinctively; he watches his tails flapping and swinging; it's so full of life in that body now, no one in the whole bloody world could ever say Dean Winchester is not alive, or that this is not life. They're going to have so much more fun together, exploring this side of life that Crowley has been on for a thousand years, and finally, one day, Dean comes to join.

"Open your eyes, Dean. See what I see. Feel what I feel. And let's go take a howl at that moon."

It's a miracle, really, watching the miraculous Dean Winchester opening his eyes. And they're black.

"Welcome back," Crowley smiles at him, staring into his eyes.

"Wow," Dean says. Which can probably be documented in the book of the most famous people's first words after rebirth.

"I know, it doesn't quite feel right at first, but you'll get used to it. And it will feel fantastic." Crowley nods in understanding.

Dean's still watching him without blinking -- though demons don't technically need to blink anyway, but still. "No, I mean, wow." Dean takes a breath (which he also doesn't need now) and exhales the exclamation.

"I mean, you."

He finally starts to make sense now, Crowley thinks. But he still hasn't quite figured out what Dean's talking about.

Crowley just tilts his head and squeezes his smile a bit harder. "I know, I'm professional at sitting at ICU bedside and waiting for people like you bounce back. Part of the job description really, who doesn't like a deal for a miracle?"

Dean doesn't answer, pushing himself up from the bed, his eyes scanning Crowley up and down like he's never seen him before. It's not disgust in his eyes, Crowley notices. His hands are still unconsciously clenching on the First Blade.

"You have friggin' horns and wings, and... and, is that a tail?" Dean widens his eyes. "What the hell? And how am I alive?"

"Oh yeah, about that, thought we'll get to that later, but now that you noticed --" Crowley makes a rough gesture of waving a hand across his head and behind his back. "There are two tails, actually, you too. And, yes, welcome to Hell. You're a demon now, Dean, so welcome to the club. Now you see me, for real, for the first time. Impressive, isn't it? But we'll talk about the fancy new attachments later, first thing first, you gotta bail, or your brother is going to charge in here in about ten minutes and shower you with a holy water shower -- I know it because that's all the patience Moose ever has for me, or any demon, why would you think he'd be any different to you?"

Dean just stares at him when Crowley is talking. And then he looks down, seeing the blade he's holding in his arms, and then glances at the mark on his right arm, and then behind his back.

"Okay," he says, pulling himself off the bed. The wings are making his moves clumsy. Crowley gives him a hand. "Where to?"

"My place. We always have a demon-onboarding 101 tutorial there," Crowley answers easily.

"Seriously?" Dean raises his eyebrows.

"'Seriously?'" Crowley rolls his eyes. "No, genius, not really. Personalized one-to-one tutoring about wings and tails and all the basics from yours truly, how about that?" He glances at the door's direction, his hearing already allows him to hear Sam's footstep heading down the hallway. "Now chop chop, you want to meet your brother like this or what? If you go with me, we're going to have so much fun and, for real, go take a howl at that moon like we talked about."

Dean jumps off the bed and tucks the First Blade behind his belt. He doesn't even take a second to think. "I need a pen," he says.

"And some paper, yes, of course, always leaving a lovely little note." Crowley snaps his fingers, and pen and paper appear in Dean's hand. And he just takes the liberty to make Dean's bed, too. Dean doesn't seem to notice.

"You have about five, four, three --" Crowley says, as he reaches out a hand. They can both hear the approaching footsteps outside the door now.

Dean scribbles the words in such a rush that even he can barely recognize the words, but that's the best he can do.

Leaving the note folded and laying on his neat white pillow, he turns back to Crowley just as he counts down to "one". "Let's go," he says with a nod.

Crowley's hand touches his shoulder before he finishes the sentence, and the two of them are landed thousands of miles away when he begins the first syllable of "Whoa."

"I'm not gonna be your free ride every time, Dean." Crowley releases his hand, but not quite leaving Dean's side. His palm brushes the air near Dean's wings, and he's sure Dean can feel the changes in the air. "You'll have to learn how to fly."
