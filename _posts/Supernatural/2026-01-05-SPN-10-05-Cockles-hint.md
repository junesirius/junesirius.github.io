omg I feel I'm so slow at taking the writer's hint.

so for S10E05 *Fan Fiction*, the scene with the girls playing Dean and Cas being a real couple in that world, I always just read it as a hint for destiel (as Marie also just quickly mentioned they'll explore in act 2 of their musical). But really, meta-narratively, if the musical version of spn is a mirror to the real spn show, then the real world outside the musical is a mirror to the real world of our world. Which is literally saying, Siobhan and Kristen are a real couple is just a crazy in-world hint -- not even that much of a subtext now, a very on-the-nose text -- that Jensen and Misha are a real couple in the real world. It's not (just) about destiel, it's about jenmish.

And then, Marie starts talking about the subtext of destiel "you can't spell subtext without S.E.X." *while glancing at Siobhan and Kristen*. So this is literally shoving the explicit truth that cockles have had sex to the face of Dean (Jensen) and he just took it.

My brain is exploding. How can I never realize that? This is literally the writer throwing the big fat truth at us: destiel is jenmish's fault and they're very much real couple. Holy, they really did it.

<br>

BTW, here's what's in the script for that clip:

> Dean looks away... just in time to see the girls playing Castiel (Kristen) and Dean (Siobhan). **They're also standing close together.** **Kristen caresses Siobhan's cheek. Reminder... they're in costume-- they're dressed as Dean and Castiel. They pull each other tight, into a warm hug.**
>
> **Dean:** Okay... what are *they* doing?
>
> **Marie:** Um, the kids today call it hugging.
>
> **Dean:** *That's* part of the show?
>
> **Marie:** No. Siobhan and Kristen are a couple in real life. Although, we do explore the nature of Destiel in Act Two.
>
> **Dean:** I'm sorry: what?
>
> **Marie:** Just subtext. But then again: you can't spell subtext without S.E.X.
>
> OFF DEAN... starting to clue in...
>
> **Dean:** *What*?

<br>

For some reason, probably because of the shooting angle, I was never sure if the girls were really just hugging or if they also kissed, but from the script, it's very clear that that's a very tight, warm hug. Like the kind Dean and Cas always have.

Another interesting thing is, here the script and show both have the she-Cas being the one reaching out first, caressing the she-Dean's cheek and hugging, showing that Cas/Misha is the more proactive one between the two, and the mutual touch-starvation they have to touch and hug together, and that Dean/Jensen would always reciprocate and hug back. well, certainly not wrong, on screen or on stage, everyone has eyes.
