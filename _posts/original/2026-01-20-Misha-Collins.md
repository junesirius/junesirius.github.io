God, I miss him so much.

I miss him so much already, right after I saw him.

And every minute away from the time I met him, it's getting worse.

God, I had sworn I wouldn't do this again. I swore I wouldn't fall in love with a celebrity again. I swore to myself, they're just an illusion, a perfected version of my desire's incarnation. I'm just projecting. They're imperfect. He's imperfect.

Goddammit I *know* he's imperfect. He can get so unhinged and disgusting and so political to the degree of annoying. I told myself, after last time hurt so badly and traumatized by Jared, I told myself I won't do this again. I told myself all I love is the character, I love Cas, I don't love him. I don't. I told myself I won't, and that's the safest way.

Then I got impulsive to buy the ticket when he *messaged* me about the show. I just couldn't miss it. I just couldn't not see him, at least for once. I told myself, I'll just go to see the show, see what he'll talk about. I saw the reviews and I lowered my expectations. I told myself maybe this is just one of his many dirty joke sessions. I know how unhinged he could get, so I'm just expecting dirty jokes and nothing more. So I won't get disappointed whatever he said.

Then I got fidgety over the whole weekend and the whole day. Knowing today's working meeting wouldn't end until four almost destroyed me. I thought no way I'll get there in time now. Left the office at 4:15, and the google estimation is 2 hours and 45 minutes. I don't even have one single minute to spare.

It's the first time I drove reckless. I shouldn't, I know, but I couldn't help it.

I have to remind myself to breathe, all the 100 miles' way.

The traffic was terrible, awful, the worst ever.

I was praying to Cas to help me get there on time. I'll need a miracle to not be late. I'll need a miracle to find a parking spot.

The original plan was to leave the office by 2pm, the plan before that was to take the whole day remote and work from Irvine. The plan was to wait in the line since 5pm.

But then, I got there by 7:03. I sat down at 7:05. He didn't come out until 7:08. I made it. And it must be Cas's help.

And there he was, standing on the stage, in his beautiful blue shirt and blue jeans, against a blue, blue velvet stage curtain.

He looked astonishingly good.

I know he looks great, I know. But I warned myself, those are just photos. People do the photo-editting all the time. I warned myself, don't expect too much.

But he looked so much better in person than any photo or video or film I've ever seen.

He looked real and beautiful and dazzling. I couldn't think of anything else, I couldn't stop staring at him, not blinking. I held my breath. He's so so beautiful. I'm so distracted that even focusing on his talk becomes difficult.

Then he talked. And my heart ached for him.

I knew there would be some traumatizing therapeutic stories, I knew. But that's not I expected to hear. His childhood, his family, his parent, his upbringing, then his career, his kids. His life.

He tore himself apart and showed us his past. The painful, awful, aching past.

I laughed at his jokes, but my heart broke for his pain.

This is not just a show, it's his healing process. He might be profiting from this, but that's not the point. Bundled with the creation convention, he gives this show ten times a year. And ten times a year, he's retelling the same childhood stories, trying so hard to tell people, to tell himself, that he has grown out of it, that he has grown up and become good and better, but inside, just the fact that he *needed* to have this show, he *needed* to tell all those strangers -- us -- about his pain, shows he's still healing. This is his healing, and he's still hurting.

I ached for him. And I see him more now.

Vulnerability makes you feel intimate.

He makes himself vulnerable, telling us the life and time when he was at his lowest point of his life. And that, that alone, makes this shared moment so, so intimate.

That must be why I fell deeper.

Then, the stupid, stupid me failed to find the stage door. I should have asked the security, I should have followed the other girls, I had so many chances, but just the sight of the security scared me -- just another trauma from the awful awful experience with Jared that I doubt I would heal ever again -- and the midnight dark LA street scared me.

Once in a lifetime's chance (maybe in a year or two, actually? maybe, hopefully), and I missed him.

In my mind's eye, I'm rewriting my memory. In the alternate universe, I see myself follow the other girls, waiting for him by the stage door. I see him come out in his beautiful deep blue sweater. I see him greet us one by one, smiling and chatting with us. I see him register my Castiel costume, looking into my eyes, smiling at me, telling me he likes it. I see myself ask if I can get a hug, and I see him hugging me so tight, my head rubbing his. I see myself ask if I can get a photo, and I see him take my phone and shoot one for me. I see myself ask if I can have a sign in the book of his poetry, and I see him sign on the first page, signing it to me. I see them so clearly in my mind's eyes, and replay them over and over again, overwriting the old memory. This, this is my new memory now. This must be what really happened. In one of the other universes. This me just so happened to live in this universe where I didn't have all of these.

I'm still so happy for all the greetings and compliments from all the other fans, telling me my Cas costume is beautiful, telling me they like it. I love them, I love all of them. The fellow fans are the best part of the world. I love the girl who showed me her Enochian tattoo, it's so beautiful and awesome. I should have asked for her contact. Why didn't I?

I didn't realize the pain and aching until the old lady so kindly told me he had left, and that I should drive safe, and that the Cas costume is the best. I didn't know how bad the aching would become, until I got into the car and started driving back. A hundred miles to get back. And every mile, the aching got worse.

It started as a disappointment that I didn't allow myself to feel. *Nothing to be disappointed about,* I told myself. It's not a guarantee to have him by the door anyway. I just bet on the wrong door. It happens.

I'm upset for missing him. I'm upset for not seeing him for longer time, for the three-hour rushed drive that could have killed me, for the sacrificed team building dinner that I didn't know until this morning and I knew I wouldn't really enjoy anyway. I couldn't leave the work meeting early, but I'm willing to skip the social aspect, for him. That's my choice and sacrifice, for him. And I hoped I could get more out of this. And I'm trying to tell myself, don't be.

Don't be. And I did get over it rather quick.

But then, instead, it's replaced by aching. My heart ached so much that I can taste all the bitterness and pain. For the first time, I realized "aching" isn't just a phrase, "bitter" isn't just a phrase. I feel like I'm mourning, for every minute I couldn't see him. "It's scary, not seeing him," just like that famous Jenmish video from fifteen years ago.

I'm mourning for missing him, I'm mourning for loving him but I know that's all I can get. He has people he loves, and he'll up there, on the stage, and never going to know my name and never going to reply my message and never going to love me back. He has his girlfriend, he has Jensen, he has his friends, his world. A whole different world.

I see now why Jensen loves him so much. I'm seeing the person Jensen loves, and is loved back. And so genuine and real. Even though I tried to tell myself he's an actor, it'd be so easy for him to fake it, so easy to lie and perform and just takes it as a job. But I'm trusting him to be real, I'm trusting him to be genuine. I'm trusting that, that's exactly why Jensen loves him so much, and everyone else.

I can feel the love in my heart, growing and growing, so strong it's out of my control. It's been a long long time since I felt love so strong, if ever. Never to any person real and reachable, that much I do know.

Love is painful.

It's pining.

It's longing.

It's everything that I can't have.

And how come there's never anyone I can have.

The dating app doesn't work. The real people in the world I get to run into doesn't work. They just never bloody work.

I didn't really even want much.

But I cried when I see the version of him giving me a tight hug. I cried. The touch starvation is getting worse.

I'm starting to get confused, do I really cry for not having him but painfully in love with him, or do I cry for not having anyone in my life that I can have a hug like this, and I can love and be loved like this. And I'm jealous that he gets to have it.

I want. I want. I want. Goddammit, I desire, I love, I ache, I pain, I cry.

God, tell me, why do I have to know that I *can* feel the love in my heart, only to know I can't have it. Not from anyone, from him, or find the right person.

Right when I thought I didn't have any real romantic attraction or sexual attraction, when I accepted the fate of being an aro-ace, that the feeling of love hits me so strong it tears me apart.

Love is painful. Whoever it's aimed for. Wherever it comes from.

You know it's love when it hurts. The bad it hurts, the stronger the love is.

God, I didn't realize how much I love him. To be honest, I still don't think it's him that I'm in love with. Just an idea of someone I can love, just the incarnation of love itself. Love the love itself, but too abstract to find the real flesh and blood to hold on to. *That*, that's where the pain and ache from, that's where the mourning is from. I'm mourning for never able to find that for real. I still believe love like this can still exist, just from the aching in my heart, I know it exists, but I'm mourning, for I don't believe it will happen to me.

God, fucking Misha, look what you've done to me.

I'm a wreck.

The only good thing is probably just, this is exactly the right amount of pain and aching and pining and love that I'll need for my next story. I'll just, channel all of me, this piece of my soul, into that story, into Dean and Cas, into my characters. That's what I do. That's the only way I can handle this. That's probably the only good and use those feelings can bring about.

I still want so desperately to see him again.
