It feels like things are falling apart. Everything. And the only thing holding them together, is me, with my body, holding the strings. Like the early spider man with the broken ship. Except it's not me holding the strings, it's the strings holding me, tying me up, putting me in the middle, pulling me apart. Like Dean, when he's in Hell.

I'm holding them too tight, or they're holding me too tight. I can't tell. I'm too afraid to let any of the strings go, too afraid for the things I'm holding together crumble.

"Let them go," everyone who loves me tells me so, "you're hurting yourself. At least let some of them loose."

But I'm too scared to, it's easier to keep holding on everything, keep imagining I can do this, I can do everything I want, if only I just work a little harder, push a little further, stay a little later, I can achieve everything, I can keep everything in place. The strings are tightening, cutting into flesh and bones, stressing, tensing, stretching, killing. I'm bleeding, I'm exhausted, I'm breaking apart, I'm immobilized, I'm frozen, I'm dying.

Funny, even then, I'm keeping the surface normal and together. Got the comment just this past week, they say, "For someone breaking down, you're oddly held together." I guess I did, after all, one of the strings is to keep the facade.

*Let some of the strings go, spider man.* I hear the voice in my head telling me. It's survival now. I wouldn't survive this, my own ambition, my own high hope, my own wish that I'm something I guess I'm not. Let myself go.

I guess, now I start to see what it is that I'm so scared of: knowing there is something I can't do, knowing I'm not as much as I hope I am, knowing I'm letting me down, knowing, after all, I'm just normal plain human being with all the things I can't do, that the dreams and hopes and wishes, are only just that. But still, disappointment is better than killing myself for them. I guess.
