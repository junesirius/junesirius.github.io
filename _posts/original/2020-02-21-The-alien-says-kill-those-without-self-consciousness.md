An alien landed on Earth.

Plants repel violently, stones sneer loudly.

The world of humans is in a mess.

The right of existence is doubted.

...

The Chain Reaction is taking effect, and it's difficult to decide which of these events is more catastrophic.

Wait, wait, wait, let's pause for a little bit here and turn the clock ten hours back to understand what's happening.

<br>

Everything big started with something small, which, at the beginning, might even be too unimportant to be noticed. In this circumstance, it started with a meteor shower. Hundreds of thousands of comets slid through the dark sky, leaving bright tails behind them, and died as cold black meteorites falling onto the earth. However, for some reason, one of them was not killed by the burning and falling. It just passed out, and landed on a street in Los Angeles.

It looked just like a common unnoticeable black stone, felt solid and cold just like a stone if you touch it, sounded dreary just like a stone if you knock it. In a word, you couldn't possibly tell it from another normal, ordinary, lifeless, black stone.

Except it had self-consciousness.

So maybe we should call it an alien now, since it was something from outer space and had self-consciousness.

<br>

At first, nobody noticed anything unusual. The stone - the alien - woke up from the coma two hours after its landing. Stones don't have senses, so this stone alien, too, couldn't see, or hear, or touch. But it had self-consciousness, and was able to use its mind to communicate and pass information. Right now, there were too many thoughts and consciousness flowing around it, which was so damn noisy that this was the exact source why it woke up from the coma.

"*Hey, you,*" the stone alien called out to a passing thought.

The owner of this thought, a male human, just passed by the stone, and heard an unfamiliar voice popping out of nowhere inside his head. He looked around, and saw nobody seemed to be hearing and speaking in that strange voice. The voice sounded chilly and stony in his head. He shook his head, thinking it must be a hallucination due to a lack of rest recently. He walked away quickly, and his walking pace was not even slowed down a bit by this small interlude.

A female adult passed by next, with a similar reaction to this new voice inside her mind. And later, another male elder. He walked away slower, but he was walking away nonetheless, looking not even remotely surprised, as if he was used to hearing voices and sounds in his head.

And then a cat. The cat also heard the strange stony voice inside its head when it passed by the stone, speaking in its familiar catty native tongue. The cat stopped curiously and found the stone, sniffed it, licked its rugged surface, played with it in its paws, and then was quickly distracted by a shiny flying beetle that was way more interesting, without listening carefully to what the stone wanted to say next.

<br>

"*Everything! Attention!*" After being neglected for about two hours, the stone alien growled impatiently in the head of whoever was within a radius of fifty feet of it. "*I'm here with a mission.*"

People around it stopped and began murmuring. Their voices were quiet, but their thoughts spoke so loudly that the stone alien could hear everything. Most people were trying to figure out the source of the voice in their heads, but they were asking the wrong question.

"Did you hear that? I'm not crazy, am I?" a husband asked his wife in a panic, "Who's speaking? And doesn't he mean 'everyone' instead of 'everything'? Isn't that strange?"

The wife nodded in just the same amount of confusion and panic.

Their baby in the stroller started crying. The wife and the mother was immediately occupied by the more important task and stopped thinking about the voice.

<br>

But human beings are not the only species that could hear the stone alien's message; **everything** with self-consciousness could. The problem is, who and **what** has self-consciousness?

"*My mission is,*" said the stone alien coldly, "*to kill those without self-consciousness. So now, prove to me that you have self-consciousness, or you will die. How do you know your behavior is not controlled or pre-programmed? Show me, persuade me, or you die.*"

A strong wave of panic rose among the human crowd, and so did sarcastic sneers.

<br>

"Who do you think you are?" said one arrogantly, "How can you possibly kill anyone if you don't even dare to reveal yourself?"

His death came quickly as he asked, as if a demonstration. But in other people's eyes, the process seemed like a strange slow-motion film. Just like some silly old show, or some exaggerated cheap comedy show, his face showed an expression of confusion, and then shock, then anger, as if he was arguing with someone or something inside his brain.

He didn't say anything out loud, so nobody else knew what was actually taking place. In the end, he screamed, "No, no, no, no, no."

With his hands holding his head as if it hurt so much that it was going to explode, he kneeled and fell down onto the ground. The scream lasted for another several minutes and then stopped promptly. He had no breath or heartbeat by then.

"Jesus Christ." The surrounding crowd gasped in horror, but no one could explain or possibly guess how it happened. And no one knew how to protect themselves from the mysterious killer, or to answer the challenge: "Prove that you have self-consciousness." It might as well ask a philosophy major student to do an impromptu in-class exam and recite the complete Meditations. It wasn't like people walking on the street everyday thinking about their own consciousness and self-consciousness and subconsciousness.

But the threat of death was real and imminent. Panic was spreading tremendously fast. So was the demand of the alien.

Soon it was more than fifty feet. It became fifty yards, then fifty miles.

Somebody posted this "collective illusion" online, along with the photo and verbal recap of the mysterious death. The tag "# selfConsciousnessOrDeath" went viral in the blink of an eye, trending incredibly fast until it became the top topic on every media. The internet spread the surprising news to the whole world within the next two hours. It became what everyone in the whole world was talking about.

News reporters, scientists, psychologists, philosophers, sociologists, sci-fi fans, and many more policemen, all came to the scene. And of course, the stone alien still lay in the place where it fell six hours ago.

<br>

"This is insane." A famous scientist couldn't stop himself from drawing this conclusion, after examining the elements of the stone carefully, analyzing everything that was analyzable, but found nothing physically special.

"This is insane." A philosopher talked with the stone alien using mind communication, and left before he was killed, but someone reported he was driven mad some time later.

"This is insane." Everyone said so. A stone, as an alien, had the mission to distinguish things with and without self-consciousness and kill the latter? What's impossible now? People might not be surprised when the sun turns green or the earth decides not to obey the law of gravity.

But the most hilarious and sarcastic fact is that, they still have to follow the game rule of the stone alien.

<br>

A rose died quietly in a nearby garden for no good reason, just like the first man. Its petals shivered a few times, and then the whole flower withered, but its neighboring flowers were still blooming. Mice climbed out of sewer pipes, and squeaked around the stone alien. It seems that after some communication or negotiation, the stone alien and the mice reached some agreements, so all the mice ran away from the stone alien and climbed back to their home in the sewer pipes.

<br>

"*Humans are so noisy,*" complained the stone alien in a lazy voice in everyone's brain, "*a lot more noisy than mice, definitely more annoying than grasses and flowers. Stones are my favorite. Talking with their consciousness is most pleasant. Tsk, tsk.*" The unsettling voice that followed sounded almost like a human clicking their tongue in disgust.

<br>

People were discussing either loudly with each other, or loudly in their own minds with the voice.

"The world is definitely mad. I mean the whole universe."

"How can mice and roses prove their self-consciousness, yet we can't?"

"Wait, did it just say, plants, animals, and even non-living things like stones have self-consciousness??"

Someone just realized something. And if it was a riddle, the answer might have just been found.

<br>

"Can you tell us how other species proved it?" A brave man thought loudly and directly to the stone alien.

"*No, I can't.*"

Well, just as expected.

"Can you share my consciousness with them and help us talk?"

"*Yes, I can, but I won't. And by the way, I'm afraid they don't have any interest in your little arrogant talks. Plants are trying to repel their roots to enable movements, animals mostly have their independent ideas, and stones just want to be left alone, so if you disturb them, you'll realize they are all very good at cursing.*"

"Can **you** prove that you have self-consciousness?"

"*Yes.*"

"How?"

"*Of course I won't tell you, won't I? That's cheating. I'm the one asking the question here.*"

"How can you tell that I don't have self-consciousness? Otherwise your proposition is proved already."

"*Oh, come on. Word game is boring.*"

"Answer me."

"*Because it is true, obviously.*"

The brave man paused, trying to think of a rational argument to counter this increasingly ridiculous debate.

"*How can you know you are not programmed to say whatever you are going to say, to think whatever you are thinking right now, even including the part you thought you were possibly programmed?*" the stone alien asked aggressively.

"Are we really talking about free will or self-consciousness here?" the brave man tried, spotting something.

"*Two very close concepts, indeed. Easy to make mistakes, but I doubt you could prove either. Just like robots, but I **know** even some robots have self-consciousness. Poor human, never find out they're never making choices or living the life on their own. Do you mind me taking your life now?*" The stone alien faked some compassion in its tone of thoughtfulness.

"No -- I don't want to die." The brave man thought desperately, yelling the words out loud.

"*Well, that one sounds closer to a real, authentic, genuine self-consciousness I'm looking for now, but still, not strong enough. You can always be programmed to be scared of death.*"

"*So -- sorry, goodbye.*"

The brave man, just like the first arrogant man, dropped down to the ground lifelessly.

<br>

Silence existed in the stone alien's mind for a moment. And then --

"You know, you made a big mistake." A voice came into the midst of the stone alien, from the place where the brave man's corpse lay.

"*What?*" It asked in a harsh voice in mind.

"You split the life with the mind." Abundant thoughts were still pouring out from the gradually colder and stiffer body.

"Thanks for your hint, the one with the stones. It actually gave me the idea, the true inspiration to find the answer. You took life away from the living things that couldn't prove their self-consciousness, but you couldn't take the consciousness away from them, and you couldn't kill them twice. So, according to you, the thoughts, my thoughts and consciousness, would remain after I was killed. Therefore, without programming in my living body, my self-consciousness still exists, which is the same self-consciousness either pre-death or post-death. Which is the same as the stones, dead or alive; or humans, dead or alive. QED."

"But pity, why did no one think of the importance of death when they tried to solve the riddle and prove it? I was so close to the answer at the last minute of my life, yet I still wouldn't solve it until I died."

"And if my guess is correct, you were expecting no one to figure it out, and so you could gradually kill every single human being on the planet. That's your real vicious mission. But you should stop now, I solved it, I proved it, and they are like me, so it is also proved for them. You can't kill any of them now. Or anything on Earth, even just another rock."

"*Bingo.*" The stone alien listened quietly without interruption for quite a long time until now.

"It is the death that proves your self-consciousness, self-awareness, free will, or whatever terminology you call it. The death makes one realize what you choose to do or to be, who you are, how one will spend their life. It is death that gives life meaning."

"*Well said,*" the stone approved. "*Now, my mission is complete.*"

After those last words of thoughts, the stone alien became completely quiet, just like an ordinary stone. And nobody heard the stiff stony voice in their heads again ever. Well, actually maybe not really "ever", maybe still once or twice for some people (they tend to claim stones talking to them now instead of they think about the question too hard themselves), but at least the good thing is, nobody dies of the question of self-consciousness again (even when these people still find it impossible to figure the question out).

<br>

**The end**
